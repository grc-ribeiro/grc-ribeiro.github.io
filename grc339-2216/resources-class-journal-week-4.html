GRC 339: Resources

Food, sitcom, and movies

Fast and comfort food

Several of you mentioned In‑N‑Out, which I like very much, but we’re a Five Guys family since we moved to the U.S. twelve years ago. The first time my wife had their burgers at Ohio State campus got us hooked forever. But In‑N‑Out and Habit are alright too. If you’re going to Habit, you have to order the Santa Barbara Char, with avocado on a sourdough.

Somebody mentioned McDonald’s too. It’s not my first choice, but an easy solution on the road. My wife sometimes prefers it to In‑N‑Out, but I don’t.

Chipotle was surprisingly mentioned only by one person. We’re definitely a Chipotle family. We eat it literally every week.

Local taco truck was mentioned too. That’s always an excellent choice, especially in the Southwest. I’ve had very good tacos in Austin and Los Angeles. (That’s where my students from San Diego get mad at me for never trying it there. I will eventually.)

A person who loves poke said that their favorite in the area is Shin’s Poke in Pismo Beach. I’ll have to try it. I like poke too, but I usually get it from Pokirito on Foothill.

Panda Express was also mentioned. I’d never tried it until a few months ago, but it has become a good alternative to burger for us.

Somebody mentioned Ziggy’s. As you can figured, I’m not a vegetarian by any means, but I do appreciate some vegan burgers sometimes. There’s a place in Columbus, Ohio called Northstar where my wife and I used to go. They have regular burgers, but we always ordered their vegetarian burger.

Mai Thai is also good restaurant mentioned. I’m not too big in Thai food, but I’ve been to Mai Thai several times and you can’t go wrong with steak, curry, and rice. Papa's Thai's Tom Kha Gai soup was described as a “yummy coconut-based spicy and sour soup that is perfect after a stressful week of assignments”. I have to try it!

Dutch Bros coffee in Oregon was mentioned, and I’ll keep in mind when I eventually make my way to the Beaver State. Also, a “German restaurant in Oregon with the best cheese fondue”. I need their name to keep in my list. I love some fondue, but they had me at “German restaurant”.

One person mentioned the Golden Gate Market Deli in San Francisco. I don’t think I’ve been there. I’ll make sure to check it out next time I’m in the city. I love San Francisco and market deli sandwiches.

R&D Cafe was mentioned, and Google tells me it’s a Vietnamese place in San Lorenzo, which usually means good sandwiches.

Raising Cane’s, Chick-Fil-A and Popeye’s were mentioned, but I’m not a chicken fan, so I’ll have to pass.

Sitcoms

Several of you mentioned Schitt’s Creek. My wife and I highly recommend it too!

The Office is a classic and we loved it.

Friends is another classic. I saw it all several times back in the day. I don’t think it holds that well today.

How I Met Your Mother is similar to Friends, and I saw it until season 7 or 8 when Barney licks a dog in the bar. I believe that episode is well regarded as the “jumping-shark” moment of the series.

Modern Family we gave up after two or three seasons, when Mitchel gets stucked in the bathroom changing out of his Spiderman costume on Halloween.

The Good Place was not for us. We saw one or two episodes.

Kim's Convenience we finished last week. The last season as not as good as the other ones, but we enjoyed it a lot!

Some of you mentioned New Girl, which I haven’t seen. I’ll put in my Netflix list.

For some reason, I’ve never seen It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia, Arrested development, Silicon Valley, Big Bang Theory and Scrubs either.

Somebody mentioned that they I would definitely recommend Community (except season 4). I believe I gave up on season 4, so maybe I should give it another try.

And several of you couldn’t recommend anything because you don’t watch sitcoms at all. Which found a little surprising, but that’s fine.

Movies

Several of you mentioned Black Widow, but I don’t care for superhero movies, so I’ll have to pass for now. I may watch it eventually during a flight.

Somebody mentioned that Adaptation. was “a strange but enjoyable experience”. I only saw it once, in the theater, but I agree.

Slumdog Millionaire is another one that I only saw in the theater a long time ago, but I also enjoyed it too.

Parasite I saw about a year ago. Really good, although also strange.

I was born in the year that Star Wars was released. All my friends loved it in the 80s, but I was never into it.

I haven’t seen Ford vs. Ferrari and In the Heights yet, but they’re in my list.

Somebody mentioned The place beyond the pines, which I’d never heard of; I’ll check it out. Same for Flipped, Chungking Express, and A Quiet Place Part 2.

I’ll recommend Promising young woman and Palm Springs that I saw recently and nobody mentioned.
