GRC 338: Resources

Syllabus

GRC 338: Web Development and Content Management

Course description

Web development production techniques including creating, repurposing, and managing assets for web publishing. Digital content management for tablet and mobile device publishing.

 

Course objectives

To use HTML and CSS to prototype and implement web sites.

To build responsive and responsible websites, according to the best practices in the field.

To apply design principles to web development.

To research and compare different solutions in the field of web development.

Readings

Required book: Lidwell, William, et al. Universal Principles of Design: 125 Ways to Enhance Usability, Influence Perception, Increase Appeal, Make Better Design Decisions, and Teach through Design. The book can be read online for free through the Cal Poly library.

Additional online articles will be assigned on the class website.

 

Course expectations

The class is being held asynchronously, which means we don’t have a particular time to meet during the week. You are, however, expected to be engaged with the class during the week. Please check Canvas for all the deadlines. On Mondays, Tuesdays, and Wednesdays, the discussion for the design principle assignment will be open. Your participation is required.

 

Email communication

Please note that I only respond to emails that are professionally written, with correct spelling, and addressed properly. Though we are a pretty casual group in this department, please don’t address your professors by the last name only, because this is rude. I would prefer that you call me by my name, which is Bruno. Or, if you prefer the more formal approach, please call me Mr. Ribeiro or Professor Ribeiro, but again, mind the spelling.

I will make every effort to respond to your email within 24 hours during the week, but I don’t usually respond to emails on weekends. Also, I usually don’t do critique or review through email, but since this class has been moved to an emergency remote environment, I am more than happy to answer any question you have through email.

Alternatively, you can send me a message through Canvas too.

 

Office hours

Since I can’t hold office hours in my office during this quarter, I’ll be available for office hours from Monday to Friday in the afternoon. Evaluation

A 100‑point scale will be applied to all assignments, with different weights, as indicated on Canvas. A 20‑point penalty will be automatically applied to late work. Another 20‑point penalty will be applied every 24 hours after the original due time.

 

Assignments

Design principles quizzes: 10%

In each of the first eight weeks of class, a quiz covering four design principles will be assigned on Canvas. You are expected to take the quiz before you engage in the discussion for the design principle assignment of the week. You will not be allowed to submit it later, except for the rare cases of extenuating circumstances. Make sure to plan ahead.

Design principles examples: 15%

For the first eight weeks of class, a group activity will take place on Canvas to discuss different design principles. The groups will be randomly assigned on Canvas. Each student is expected to work on the quiz and then discuss examples with the group. If I don’t see any participation in the group discussion board by Tuesday night, I will drop you from the group and you will get a zero for the activity of the week. You will not be allowed to submit it later, except for the rare cases of extenuating circumstances. Make sure to plan ahead. But in the end of the quarter, you will discard your lowest two grades for these activities.

Topic readiness: 10%

For the HTML/CSS part of the class, lessons will be assigned on Canvas. In the end of each lesson, take a quick quiz, to test if you are ready for HTML/CSS assignment of the week. You will not be allowed to submit it later, except for the rare cases of extenuating circumstances. Make sure to plan ahead.

HTML/CSS: 15%

For the first four weeks of class, we will be working on weekly assignments to exercise the concepts covered in the Canvas lessons.

HTML/CSS website: 15%

The first big project for this course is a website built with HTML and CSS. Instructions and interim deadlines will be provided on Canvas.

CMS website: 10%

The final project for this course is a website built using a content management system. Instructions and interim deadlines will be provided on Canvas and on the class website.

Midterm and final exams: 15%

There will be an online midterm exam in week 7 covering HTML and CSS. During finals week, an online final exam will cover the design principles. All exams are asynchronous with open book. Feel free to research as much as you need and even take in collaboration with another student.

Class journal: 10%

At the end of every week of class, you write a brief journal entry on Canvas. It should not take you more than five minutes, though. I will start every week by sharing with the class the highlights from the journal entries. Even though I will be able to know who wrote each entry, this information won’t be shared with class, and the entries will be shared anonymously. (Unless you submit a joke; jokes will be shared with credit to the student who submitted them.)

Plagiarism and originality

Please make sure the work you do for this courseis original and not a revised version of something you were working on in another course. Dishonesty, cheating, deception, or plagiarism is not acceptable. Unless otherwise stated and referenced in “fair use” copyright guidelines, it is expected that all design work be original work of the student.

Revising any existing work created by another person or student is plagiarism.

Revising any existing work created from another course is plagiarism.

Copying existing code from any source into your project is plagiarism, unless it is clearly authorized by the instructor of the class.

Inspiration code needs to be properly cited, with author(s) and source.

If you have any questions about academic integrity in relation to your work in this course, make sure to discuss them with me and/ or consult the Office of Student Rights and Responsibilities (OSRR). Plagiarism and cheating may result in expulsion from Cal Poly and every university in the CSU system.
