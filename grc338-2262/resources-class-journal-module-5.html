I am confused if the order of CSS items matters or if is just good practice to order things the way we have in class.

Good question. It does matter. What comes later override what was previously declared. But also, more specific overrides what’s less specific. So for example, if you have a color for your p, and later defines a different color for the p with a specific class, the browser will respect the one that came later and is more specific. You may get into some inconsistent behaviors between browsers if you declare the most specific first and declare something less specific later. So it’s good practice to declare from least specific to most specific. (Easier said than done.)
