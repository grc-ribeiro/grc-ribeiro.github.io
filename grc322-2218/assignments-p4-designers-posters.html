Tasks

  1. Research the life and work of the designer assigned to you.

  2. Collect images in high resolution.

  3. Design six versions of a 12″ × 18″ vertical (portrait) poster:
    You want to use three different grids with either 6, 7, 8, 9, or 10 columns.
    For each option of grid, design two layouts, with different placement of elements and different typefaces.

  4. Speaking of typefaces, make sure you research the type families you’re planning on using. You want to use well regarded type families. Once again, the excellent Fonts In Use website can be a very helpful starting point.

Requirements

  1. 12″ × 18″ vertical (portrait)

    By the way, if we’re talking about design work, first number is always the width, and second is height. But for art work, it’s the opposite. I didn’t make these rules.

  2. White background and no bleeding images

    I plan to print them eventually and bleeding colors would make it much harder.

  3. Name of the designer as the title of the poster

    Typeset in 32pt or bigger

  4. Short introductory text 150–200 words

  5. Portrait of the designer in grayscale

  6. Example of their work in colors

    Except when the original isn’t in colors, of course.

  7. Captions for all work examples

    Make sure you have proper hierarchy for those.

Optional

  1. Official URL if available

    Wikipedia article or other third‑party pages are not an official website.

  2. Designer’s Twitter and/or Instagram handle if available

    Again, only if it’s official and maintained by the designer themselves or someone on their team.

  3. Quotes from the designer

    Some designers have some interesting quotes, and they can be look nice on the poster.

Evaluation

  • Research

    10 points

    Is the research about the designer complete? Did you include the most important facts? Did you proofread your text?

    • 10 pts Satisfactory
    • 8 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 5 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Typography

    30 points

    Are the typefaces appropriate? Does the visual hierarchy reflect the content hierarchy? Do the paragraph lines have an appropriate length? Is the type size adequate for legibility? Is the leading adequate for the type size?

    • 30 pts Satisfactory
    • 25 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 15 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Images

    10 points

    Good selection of images that are representative of the designer’s work. Do the images have good resolution and good quality? Are they properly cropped?

    • 10 pts Satisfactory
    • 8 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 5 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Grid

    20 points

    Are all the elements properly aligned to the grid? Is the grid being well used to convey planning, intelligibility, clarity, orderliness, and credibility? Elements must be very well aligned within the grid making the layouts organized but also visually interesting.

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Color and consistency

    10 points

    Is color being used effectively to direct users to attract attention, group elements, indicate meaning, and enhance aesthetics? Are similar parts related? Are related parts similar? Are the variations consistently applied in all parts of each poster? (Posters don’t need consistency in the set, only within each layout. Don’t even think of them as a set, they are supposed to be different; see next criteria.)

    • 10 pts Satisfactory
    • 8 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 5 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Variation

    20 points

    Did you explore enough variation between layouts? Did you explore different colors, type families, grids, image sizes and placement, etc.? You don’t need different type families for every single layout, but you want to explore different ideas.

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

This first submission will count towards your final grade for the course.

Tips

  1. Titles

    Even though you’re concerned with legibility, it’s not a bad idea to experiment with typography on the title of the poster, at least in one or two layouts.

  2. Paragraph typography

    Make sure you have a legible typeface. But also, you want it to be black; you want a solid color on your small letters. And don’t forget to check for line length.

  3. Captions

    First, don’t forget the captions. But make sure it’s different than the paragraph. It’ll probably be on a smaller size, and then may be better suit with a narrower text‑column.

Submission

  1. Export each of your layout twice, once showing the grid, and once without it.

  2. Make a 6‑page PDF document with all the pages exported, with the grids hidden. Name it using the name convention lastname-grid-hidden.pdf.

  3. Make a 6‑page PDF document with all the pages exported, with the grids showing. Name it using the name convention lastname-grid-show.pdf.

  4. Submit both files to Canvas.
