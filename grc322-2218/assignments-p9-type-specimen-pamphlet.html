GRC 322: Assignments

Type specimen (pamphlet)

In typography, type specimen is a presentation of a typeface or type family to showcase its design and/or its use.

For this assignment, you will design a type specimen pamphlet for a 21st‑century type family of your choice.

Type family requirements

  1. At least two weights with their italics. (Four variations at least. e.g.: Regular, Italic, Bold, Bold Italic, etc.)

  2. Upper and lowercase.

  3. Designed and published in the 21st century (or very close to it).

  4. Make sure to find its history, designers, and plenty of examples. (If you can’t find them, there’s probably a reason.)

  5. Choose a type family that you did not work on the poster. (You’re probably tired of those two anyway.)

Tasks

  1. Research type specimen in general. (Emigre and Type Specimens are good starting points.)

  2. Research type families. (Fonts In Use and Adobe Fonts are good starting points.)

  3. Choose a type family and collect examples of its use in a platform such as Pinterest, Canva, Adobe Sparks. (More on this below)

  4. Design an eight‑page pamphlet showcasing the type family of your choice, using your collection of images as a direct inspiration for your visual language. But your collection is only for inspiration, though. You don’t want to use examples from your collection, though. (More on this below.)

  5. Check spelling and grammar. I suggest Grammarly.

  6. Export as PDF.

Image collection

For any design project, you want to start with a collection of reference images. I used to collect them in a folder on my computer, but today we have online tools. I use Pinterest.

I know most people use Pinterest to look for images, but that’s not how you should use it. Look for images in more credible sources—such as design magazines or renowned designers’ portfolios—and save on specific collections. (Beyond research for each project, I have ongoing collections loosely organized by themes.)

You want to collect images as references too. You will start by collect examples of the typeface being used in different design projects. Then, you will expand your visual research with images that match a visual language direction, even if not necessarily using the type family you’re showcasing.

The idea of the image selection is to create a mood board, where you create a visual language that would be a good fit for your type family. Is it traditional? Serious? Fun? Powerful? Delicate? Sophisticated? Provoking? (You wouldn’t use Bodoni to convey a grunge vibe or Interstate with baroque aesthetics.)

With this collection, you’ll be able to communicate a vision for your project and to establish a direction for your visual language. Then, not only will it be easier for you to design, but it will also make it easier for me to help you achieve your vision for the project.

You want between 20 and 70 images in your collection.

The pamphlet

As you can see researching type specimens, the goal is not to teach the details of the type family. You want to showcase the family so one would be inspired to buy and use it.

You’re not required to use any images on the pamphlet. It can be only type. But if you do use images, they’re not examples of use by others. You may use a illustration or photographs of the ocean, or buildings, or whatever that you think you’d go well together with the type family you’re showcasing. But typography needs to be your own work.

Download the examples from Emigre to have a better understanding what this type specimen thing is.

Requirements

  1. Eight facing pages, 5½×8½″ each.

  2. Brief history (one paragraph) of the typeface on page 3, with at least designers, type foundry, and year.

  3. Showcase at least four different fonts in the same type family, with upper and lowercase, as well as some punctuation and numerals. (Helvetica Light and Helvetica Bold are two different fonts in the same type family.)

  4. Your name on page 8.

  5. On the other pages, you want to include character, words, sentences, or paragraphs using the type family. (Again, download the examples from Emigre.)

Evaluation

You will be evaluated on your use of references (image collection) and your type skills.

  • Image collection (references)

    20 points

    Did you collect enough images with and without the type family? Do they form a cohesive collection with at least 20 images? Are the images from renowned source of good design? Did you make sure to use the references in your layout? A mood board does you no good if you don’t use it.

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially staisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • History

    10 points

    Are the facts context for the type family properly described and explained on the page? Did you include type designer, year, and location?

    • 10 pts Satisfactory
    • 5 pts Partially staisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Color and visual language

    30 points

    Does the visual language appropriately reflect the references on the image collection? Do the colors effectively enhance the quality of the typeface?

    • 30 pts Satisfactory
    • 25 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 15 pts Partially staisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Typeface showcase

    20 points

    Can we have a good sense of the type family? Did you show the main characters? Are we able to see some nice details in its design?

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially staisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Typography

    20 points

    Are the typefaces appropriate? Does the visual hierarchy reflect the content hierarchy? Do the paragraph lines have an appropriate length? Is the type size adequate for legibility? Is the leading adequate for the type size?

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially staisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory
