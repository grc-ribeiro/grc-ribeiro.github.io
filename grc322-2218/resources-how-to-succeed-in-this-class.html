Welcome letter

Welcome to GRC 322 (Advanced Digital Typography). The class begins on September 20th and ends on December 10th, when the final project and final exam will be due.

In this class, you will work on seven different projects, and at least four of them can be portfolio pieces. (All of them can be portfolio pieces if you know how to present them. I can help you, of course.)

GRC 322 is a hybrid class, where the lecturer are online and asynchronous, while the labs will be in person. You are expected to work 9 hours a week in a 3-unit class, which means six hours of work outside the lab. We have consistent deliverables every week. Please check Canvas constantly and stay ahead of schedule.

 

Tips to be successful in this class

  1. Have a schedule. My classes always have a consistent week schedule so you can plan ahead.

  2. Read the assignment instructions very carefully. I suggest you to read them sooner rather than later so you have time to ask the questions you may have.

  3. If you don’t understand the assignment requirements, ask; don’t assume.

  4. Show up to office hours.

 

Office hours

If you need my help, you can use my office hours as much as you need and you can also send me an email or a message through Canvas if you prefer—but sometimes the readiness of office hours is more efficient. I will make every effort to respond to your email within 24 hours during the week, but I don’t usually respond to emails on weekends.

 

Other resources

If you have problems logging into Canvas, you want to contact Cal Poly’s Information Technology Services as soon as possible (805.756.7000).

If you have a disability for which you are or may be requesting an accommodation, you are encouraged to contact your instructor and the Disability Resource Center (805.756.1395), as early as possible in the term.

I look forward to working with you this quarter!

Bruno
