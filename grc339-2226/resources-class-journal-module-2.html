What’s the main difference between a carousel and the photo gallery? They feel somewhat the same but I know you dislike carousels.

What I refer as a carousel is the feature usually on the home page where to highlight several different stories, you have them all rotating (usually automatically) in one space. It’s not effective, because nobody watches them. Until 2013‑ish, everybody was concerned with “above the fold”, assuming (wrongly!) that users didn’t scroll. Look at the GrC’s home page, for example. Several stories in the carousel, but only the first one is really highlighted, since most users will just scroll down looking for what they want. Galleries are very different. They are a collection of images, and you are there to calmly look at one of them at your own pace. Don’t make them links to stories and don’t use automatic change, but do include thumbnails for the navigation.
