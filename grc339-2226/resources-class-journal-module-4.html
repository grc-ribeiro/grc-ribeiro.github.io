GRC 339: Resources

Food, sitcom, movies, and cities

Fast and comfort food

Several of you mentioned In‑N‑Out, which I like very much, but we’ve been a Five Guys family since we moved to the U.S. twelve years ago. The first time my wife and I had their burgers at Ohio State campus got us hooked forever. But In‑N‑Out and Habit are alright too. If you’re going to Habit, you have to order the Santa Barbara Char, with avocado on a sourdough.

Several people mentioned McDonald’s, which was kind of a surprise. It’s not my first choice, but an easy solution on the road. My wife sometimes prefers it to In‑N‑Out, but I don’t. Jack-In-The-Box was also mentioned, but I’ve only had it once a long time ago, so I can’t say much about it.

Two people mentioned Blaze Pizza. I like their pizza ok, but it takes forever. It was supposed to be fast. Somebody voted for Domino’s, which I haven’t had in forever.

Somebody mentioned that the fish and chips at Hoogies are “amazingggg”. I’ve never tried them, but I do like some fish and chips.

One person mentioned the Whole Foods Food Court. I think it’s practical, and they do offer some decent choices. But it’s not my kind of comfort food.

One person mentioned a Mediterranean place in their hometown called Flame & Skewers. I love Mediterranean food, so if I’m ever in Bakersfield again, I’ll try it. Meanwhile, I find Petra here in SLO quite good. Also, one person mentioned Cava, but I’ve never tried it.

Mr. Pickles is nice, but I haven’t been there in a while. We had it in one of our faculty meetings a few years ago.

Another hometown vote was for Mas Tacos. Google points to Sacramento. I prefere my Mexican food from closer to the border, but I’m happy with any Mexican food. Actually, I take it back. Somebody wrote that “Taco Bell is a pretty solid fast food option”. I like this person, but they are very wrong. One person said “Taco Roco in SLO or Tacos de Mexico in Morro Bay”, but I haven’t had either.

Somebody mentioned Golden Deli, but I don’t know where that is.

Somebody mentioned Papa’s Thai and there was a vote for “any Thai restaurant”. This person can’t complain about choices in San Luis Obispo.

Another generic vote was for “anywhere that had boba and popcorn chicken”. Not my thing. Nor is Chick-fil-A or Canes.

There was one person who went with a classic: “fav comfort food is grilled cheese + tomato soup!” Not a bad choice.

And finally, somebody mentioned the Chevron gas station on Santa Rosa. I’m not kidding.

Sitcoms

Some of you mentioned Schitt’s Creek. My wife and I highly recommend it too!

A lot of you mentioned New Girl, which I haven’t seen. I’ll put in my Netflix list.

The Office is a classic and we love it.

Friends is another classic. I saw it all several times back in the day. I don’t think it holds that well today.

I was never a big fan of Seinfeld. Many of my friends are. I never loved it.

How I Met Your Mother is similar to Friends, and I saw it until season 7 or 8 when Barney licks a dog in the bar. I believe that episode is well regarded as the “jumping-shark” moment of the series.

I gave up on Shameless after six or seven seasons. No show should go longer than six seasons, by the way.

I’ve seen some episodes of Parks and Rec on airplanes. It was fine for airplanes, but I didn’t love it.

Bojack Horseman has been recommended. But I don’t think it’s for me.

I’ve never heard of The Mick, Crashing, or Psych before. I’ll check them out.

I’ve never seen Arrested development, Brooklyn Nine-Nine, It's Always Sunny in Philadelphia, or Full House either.

And several of you couldn’t recommend anything because you don’t watch sitcoms at all. Which found a little surprising, but that’s fine.

Movies

Several of you mentioned the new Top Gun. I’ll see it on a flight eventually.

The new Elvis movie was also mentioned. That I may see with my wife at home.

Somebody mentioned Little Miss Sunshine. I love it. I saw it in the theaters.

Somebody mentioned Coco. I think it’s great, but I asked for no animation because I’ve seen them all. And somebody mentioned Marcel the Shell with Shoes On. (“It’s not animation; it’s stop motion”, they said. Well, I haven’t seen it, so I guess they’re right.)

Somebody wrote that The Social Network is a great movie. It isn’t.

Also mentioned: Girl in the Picture, Money Heist: Korea, Last Night in Soho, We're the Millers, Dunkirk, Molly's Game, Into the Wild, Vivarium, Knives Out, Jurassic World Dominion, How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days, Midsommar, BlacKkKlansman, Baby Driver, Hunt for the Wilderpeople, The Darjeeling Limited, Spiderman: No Way Home, and Call Me by Your Name.

I haven’t seen many movies recently, but I’ll recommend Promising young woman and Palm Springs that I saw last year.

Cities

Somebody said that they have been in Edinburgh, Scotland years ago and would like to go back. I was there not years, but decades ago (1995). Would love to go back. It’s fantastic!

My wife and I love New York City! We took our kids there in the summer of 2019. They were 4 and 6, and absolutely loved it! My dad and my sister love it too.

Florence, Italy is beautiful! But Paris is more!

Nice is a charming city in South France.

I need to go back to Amsterdam to give it a second chance. I was there in my mid‑twenties (2003), but I didn’t love it.

I’ve never been to Auckland, New Zealand, but I was in Wellington (which was also mentioned) for a conference in 2019 and it was super fun. Super cool city.

Barcelona is very touristic, but its architecture and bars are well worth it.

Several of you mentioned Tokyo, which I think that could be on top of my list too.

Other cities that were mentioned that I’ve never been to, but are in my list too are Chicago, New Orleans, Busan, Korea, Seoul, Korea, Split, Croatia, Dublin, Ireland, Lake Como, Italy, Oslo, Norway, The Hague, Netherland, Okinawa, Japan, Kyoto, Japan.

Salem, Massachusetts is not.
