GRC 347: Resources

Class journal, week 8

Seeing other examples of extension from real life and classmates helped make the process more tangible. Although, I spent the majority of lab “perfecting” just the different sock flavors.

I don’t think there’s such thing as a perfect flavor of socks. Although, I must confess it’s just an assumption since I never tried to eat them.

I thought the Market Pantry product line was a very interesting example. I never noticed how all the fonts were different, but the color scheme keeps everything cohesive.

I found it surprising and interesting when I first noticed it too.

I’m learning a lot about Adobe Dimensions with this project. Using Adobe Stock for the first time has also been interesting. I had no idea they had 3D models to use, though admittedly limiting users to 10 model downloads a month seemed a little weird.

I’m glad Adobe Dimensions is working for you. But that limitation is weird indeed.

What was the name of the application used in GrC 337 to make 3D models of packaging? Ever since the Cal Poly dashboard has been updated, I can’t go back and access previous quarters’ data and I was hoping to use that software for my line extension.

You may have learned iC3D, but I’d try Adobe Dimensions first.

I think that it is interesting to see the ways that brands differentiate between products, like light colors for “light” food items. I personally subconsciously recognized this pattern but don’t usually think about it when shopping.

Part of the design process is to make conscious decision based on audience’s unconscious understanding.

I’ve been having a bit of designer’s block in figuring out how everything will fit together, but with Dimensions its all finally coming together, especially with the unified branding. Feels good.

The visual language looks much better in 3D mockups.

Reminding myself to change very few things between varieties of the same product helped me focus on a consistent design.

Changing only as much as necessary sounds like a good way to go.

Differentiating shapes for different products and colors for flavors or scents seems to get pretty complicated pretty fast, just having 2 flavors or scents in your line can double the number of designs you have.

You don’t need to show all flavors/scents for all products, though.

I found a copy of the world’s worst thesaurus. Not only is it awful, it’s awful.

Thanks, Daniel.
