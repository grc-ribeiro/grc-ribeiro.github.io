GRC 347: Resources

Class journal, week 9

Last week was a panic. I couldn’t find my saved first draft files after I closed illustrator and I thought I lost them and would have to rework everything. When I went to restart my designs from memory, I opened an older file and I found the files I thought I lost. It turned out I did not rename my files the way I normally do, based on the assignment, instead I just saved over an old assignment. This was a relief, and I was going to put it in the journal but I missed the deadline last week. I am feeling much better about this assignment this week.

Phew. I’m glad it worked out in the end.

Been working in Adobe Dimension to try to put together a 3D line extension, but I can’t find a stand up pouch similar to the ones I’m trying to model after. I know there’s stock libraries available courtesy of Adobe, but do you happen to know of any resources I could find more 3D models to import into Dimension?

I don’t. If anybody knows, please let Daniel know.

I learned some more about Adobe Stock. Turns out the free trial isn’t so free once you reach your buy-out limit of 10 models. Kinda feels weird that a stock library would charge extra for models you’re already subscribed to use.

It really is a shame.

It’s amazing how it all comes together. Being able to build on your previous research and work is highly valuable. As you go, it becomes less and less ambiguous and more focused.

This class is quite slow-paced, but it does get you there.

It’s very hard to try not to center text on a bottle. Working on finding some left-alignment text solutions.

I don’t disagree. But it’s worth it.

When making a presentation for a philosophy class yesterday, I had all of the slide titles in sentence case, and then one of my group members went through and capitalized everything. I guess that’s more relevant to 322, but we are past that point. These are the last journals, huh.

That’s normal. I’m glad to hear you tried. (And, yes, we’re past that point.)

This week I worked on filling out all of the info and graphics I had left in my designs, this made me realize how much info and certification (for things like recycled content or cruelty free) would need to happen for actual products.

Depending on the industry, there’s a lot that needs to be there.

The creators of the bear-shaped honey bottles tried to make the bear look as different from Winnie the Pooh as possible in fear of getting sued.

That’s an interesting fact. I had no idea.

I’m excited to see everyone’s final products. Week 9 slump is real though.

Let’s all enjoy the break, then!
