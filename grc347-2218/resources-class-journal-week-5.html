GRC 347: Resources

Class journal, week 5

I learned I need to get better about bringing my laptop into class so I can actually work on my files. That was an oversight.

That’s what I’d do if I was a student.

It is important to see how your logo will look as a part of a design and not just floating in space.

Yes. That’s how you can evaluate it.

The visual ideation lab was a bit challenging as I found myself designing a lot of metaphorical visual identities.

It was supposed to be challenging.

This week has been extra exhausting. Coming into lab, I was not looking forward to finding my creative juices and work on the visual ideation. I feel as if I am running low on creativity, maybe it is because I need a refresher away from screens for some time. It is week 5 and I do not think I was ready for in-person again and the exhaustion that it brings.

I hear you. Try to get some quality time outdoors if you can.

It was a little difficult doing this week’s quiz. It took me a long time to think of logos that I like and then research designers. A lot of information about who designs logos is not readily available which is sad because I feel like those designers don’t get a lot of recognition. Even though it took a long time it was very interesting!

It was supposed to be challenging too.

Its so hard not to completely imitate other logo designs and styles off the internet while still using them for inspiration. Its not that I’m trying to copy them, just their most outstanding elements… this becomes difficult when there are only two or three elements present in the first place.

Start by copying, then we can improve it and make it closer to your brand values. In the end, it will be very different from the original.

Throwing a bunch of ideas down helped me make a bunch of iterations. It took me a while before I started finding ideas that I was really into. What helped even more was getting ideas from my group mates that lead to another whole set of ideas.

That’s how it’s supposed to be.

I wonder what makes arbitrary logos effective as people can be confused on what the connection it has with the company.

Any logo, arbitrary or not, can be confusing if you think about it. You just need to make sure it doesn’t convey anything contrary to your brand values.

I definitely want to check out that “Marks of Excellence” book featured in the video!

I have a copy in my office, you’re welcome to borrow it.

For next week I think I might look at the ways that my logos can be displayed - (the whole thing with the name and possible imagery vs just the imagery, etc.).

Good idea.

My next steps will be to create more designs/iconography for my packaging. I thought single colours would look good but I think it needs something more.

Maybe it does, maybe it doesn’t.

Did you know there is an actual word in the English language for pushing someone out a window? “Defenestration”. What had to happen for a singular word to be created for the action?

I actually sort of knew that. We have a similar word in Portuguese, but it can be using for throwing things out of a window too.
