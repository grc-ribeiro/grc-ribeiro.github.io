GRC 347: Assignments

Packaging design

This ten‑week project will consist in designing the packaging for a fictitious line of product from the list below:

  1. car products

    motor oil, polishers, etc.

  2. dairy products

    milk, butter, yogurt, etc.

  3. eye care

    contact solutions, contacts, etc.

  4. garden tools

    shovel, pruners, etc.

  5. hardware

    hammer, nails, etc.

  6. healthy beverage

    no alcohol or soda of any kind

  7. healthy snacks

    no sugar or artificial sweeteners

  8. home cleaning supplies

    detergents, brushes, etc.

  9. innerwear

    socks, undershirts, etc.

  10. office supplies

    pen, paper, stapler, etc., but no art supplies

  11. oral care

    tooth brushes, toothpastes, etc.

Competitive landscape

As part of the process, you need to work on a preliminary analysis of the competitive landscape. You want to search for the main brands in your field as well as the not-so-known brands, with different, nicer visual language.

Tasks

  1. Start by googling the categories above before you make a decision. (Spend a minute or two on each.)

  2. Choose two or three categories to investigate a little further. (Five to ten minutes per category should be sufficient.)

  3. Choose the category will be working on.

  4. Use Pinterest or similar service to collect the images.

    Important: Although, Pinterest is great for collecting images, it is awful tool for searching for images. Do not use Pinterest to look for your images. Find better sources and upload them to Pinterest yourself.

  5. Include somewhere from 30 to 80 images on your board. (There’s no penalty for doing more, though.)

    You want the images to be directly related to your category. You will include the main brands in the market, but you also want to include less‑known brands, with nicer packaging. And don’t forget that packaging exists everywhere in the world; look for references beyond the American market. But all images need to be package or ads for products in your category.

Evaluation

Check rubric for evaluation criteria.

(If you don’t see a rubric, please let me know as soon as possible.)

A common mistake is to treat the competitive landscape as a mood board and to include images that are not package or ads for products in your category.

Another common mistake is to choose a category that is not in the list above.
