GRC 347: Resources

Class journal, week 2

I am excited to be working with groups, it was fun to see other people building their brand voice and to help them find the words that describe their brand. I wish we met more often for this class, it feels disjointed coming in only once a week. I miss the shortened schedule from summer school with 339, but maybe that will change as the quarter goes on and my workload increases.

For these first weeks, I understand why it feels like this, since there isn’t much homework. But Once you start designing your package, you may need more time between labs.

I learned that if you make a Pinterest board private the link you submit on Canvas won’t function properly and you’ll get a temporary zero and have a temporary heart attack.

No need for heart attacks here. Just fix your mistakes and we’ll move forward.

I found it difficult to create my brand voice today because I had not given it much thought beforehand except for one initial idea. I feel like I made my pinterest board limited to that idea instead of letting myself get inspired by other images I found in my search. I just wanted it all to look cohesive right away. Coming up with words was challenging for me too. I wish I started a few words and looked for images around that instead of using the one concept I had in my head.

Maybe the process you described would work better for you. The hardest part of teaching design process is that sometimes I make it sound like it’s the only possible process, and it’s absolute not. I’m just giving you tools to work on your own process in the future. Once you try a lot of different processes, you’ll know what works best for you.

Explaining your thoughts to someone else can be helpful in solidifying your own ideas.

Absolutely! Learning by teaching beats learning by doing every day.

I learned that Pinterest is not all that reliable when trying to look up specific things, only things that are generally connected to a specific topic. I ended up getting some great stuff for my group mates, though, which was pretty neat.

Pinterest is terrible if you’re looking for images. It’s a tool to collect the images you find elsewhere.

Coming up with mood boards proved challenging. It took more effort, to find a few images that felt cohesive with the brand voice I wanted. I ended up with a niche that I think I can start with, but I want to keep my eyes open for more things that speak to me.

Yes, please do keep your eyes open. The work is not done until it’s done.

Creating a mood board with images not directly relating to the type of product you’re selling definitely seems as though it would fuel ideas moreso than just sticking with inspiration from already-made products.

Absolutely. It’s from association of ideas that new ideas will come.

I noticed that I sort of started with one idea but as I found more images and references my direction shifted a bit.

That was one of the goals.

To me, mood boards have always been stuff like colors and cool pictures of neatly decorated houses. I never really considered that fonts might work great on a mood board, which is what happened this week.

For every project, you need to work on your references first.

I like working in teams to get feedback on my ideas. I am glad everyone is so nice and helpful in this class.

That’s one thing you can often count in the GrC program, right?

I think that the most difficult part of packaging branding is coming up with an idea that stands out but does not make your product unrecognizable to consumers.

Challenging indeed. You’ll get there, but not without making adjustments.

Myself and others have found a few examples of brand like Burger King and McDonald’s changing their visual language to be more minimalist and a modern. This is kind of interesting since it just them away from the more “cheap and easy” look, but I guess a lot of other fast food chains are interested in looking less like fast food.

That’s probably it. They’re probably trying to find ways to compete with other chains that are fast and inexpensive, but don’t seem too “fast food”, like Blaze Pizza and Chipotle.

I found this cool website that features a ton of different packaging examples. Link: https://www.packagingoftheworld.com/.

Thanks for sharing, Mads!

I am very curious about some of the more vintage fonts I found while building my mood board. My friend and I have been designing a game as a side project and we’ve had trouble nailing down the overall aesthetic. However, the whole “futurepunk” and “vintage sci-fi” look has really inspired me!

That sounds fun! Let us know how it goes!
