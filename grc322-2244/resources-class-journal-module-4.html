Class journal, module 4

The critique video is helpful because not only can you learn from your mistakes, but you can see learn from your classmates’ work as well.

Of course! And it’s better than doing this in the lab, isn’t it? Let me know if you all prefer traditional critiques in‑person.

Is the font research part individual? When is it due?

Individual. Due before the lab.

Over the course of this project, I’ve realized that symmetry is boring, and I find asymmetrical layouts to be far more visually interesting.

Way more.

I find it hard to make my design asymmetrical, I always end up reverting back to making things all fit in about to or three columns.

It’s supposed to be hard. Symmetrical design is not always bad, but it’s an easy way out. It’s boring, uninteresting, and uninviting.

The horizontal grid has to start from the top margin but does not have to line up with the bottom margin, which I found interesting.

It doesn’t have to. But I usually change my bottom margin to make line up with the baseline.

This LinkedinLearning Donna suggested I watch was actually very helpful and informative: https://www.linkedin.com/learning/designing-with-grids-in-indesign-2/creating-better-designs-with-grids?u=2121556.

I didn’t know about that one. Seems pretty helpful indeed. Thanks for sharing, Isis!

This project has opened my eyes to new designers and influenced my decisions to incorporate their style of design into my personal design for the poster.

One of the goals of the project, of course, was to make you all more familiar with designers you didn’t know before. There’s a reason we do it on designers and not some other topic.

Looking for layout inspiration helped me a lot for the final design.

Always look for inspiration!

Now that it’s over I appreciate the method of making a bunch of “bad” drafts and then having several rounds of refining. It’s helpful to take the pressure off in the beginning.

I encourage you to try it on your own.

I really struggled designing this week and felt like I wasn’t getting anywhere with the problems I had, just reminding myself that the design process is not a straight line.

It really isn’t!

Annemarie and I swapped computers/designs for 10 minutes and worked on each other’s page design. This was very helpful as it provided a new perspective on our design.

I love this so much.

I overheard some people in lab who had swapped their designer posters(?), meaning they had a fresh set of eyes on each other’s layouts which seems like a good idea.

I know! It’s fantastic!

The most valuable resource has been my peers, they are very talented and inspiring!

I absolutely agree that you are the most valuable resource to each other. But I don’t love the word “talent” for designers, because it means “natural aptitude or skill”, and I don’t think it comes naturally. It takes hard work. And GrC students are smart, kind, and hard-workers.

Getting feedback from others is sooo helpful. Often other people see things you wouldn’t have noticed.

Yes. Always. And if you don’t agree with the feedback, you don’t need to act on it.

Like Bruno said, knowing that I would need to explain my design decisions encouraged me to think more about the decisions I was making. In some cases, it actually helped me to make decisions!

Sometimes, I know what I’m doing.

Articulating design choices can be hard when designing sometimes feels intuitive, but I think understanding the choices we make is important in order to grow.

Also, not all intuitive decisions are good. They may not be bad either. Make quick decisions without thinking to much. Think about them carefully before committing.

Writing about the design process made me notice details in the work that weren’t necessarily intentional but were still effective.

Now, you intentionally let them there.

As you are making changes to the layout and type, its helpful to write down your reasoning so you can look back on that for the design decisions write-up.

Good idea!

It’s much different designing when you have to justify each choice. Makes me really think about my design decisions.

Mission accomplished.

Sometimes you need to let go of a design element to make the piece work better because it is limiting you.

Yes. Break‑ups are hard, but they’re usually the best for everybody involved.

White space is so important when designing, but it is also important to make sure white space is evenly distributed throughout the design.

It’s an element just like the others.

Finding a way to create white space is harder than filling it.

Well worth it, though.

The title does not always have to be massive.

It does not.

Pull quotes are a dynamic and effective way to establish further hierarchy on a poster.

True. It can add more elements.

I enjoyed it too!
