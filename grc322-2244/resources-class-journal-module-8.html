Class journal, module 8

NO AMPERSANDS, they are SO ten years ago!

Totally.

Something interesting about the type comparison project is that I feel like my attention to detail has improved. Although the projects are about comparing visually similar typefaces, by now, I’ve spent so much time analyzing the two typefaces I worked on that I feel like I can easily tell them apart.

But the main go is not becoming able to tell those two specific typefaces apart. The goal is learning to notice details on letterforms.

The lab has seen me at my lowest point, I am always stressed out!!!

Please don’t stress out. It’s only school.

Also, I was really reminded that designing is about making choices during this project. I liked an element from one of my three ideas, but keeping it resulted in unappealing compositions, and I couldn’t find a way around that. Ultimately, I decided that the trade-off wasn’t worth it, and discarded that element in subsequent revisions.

Break‑ups can be hard. But they’re often the best outcome for everybody involved.

Don’t be afraid of discarding aspects if it doesn’t work for the whole thing.

Exactly.

Working separately in this last lab and then coming together at the end to pull the best ideas together, was much more effective in the design decision process than looking over someone’s shoulder while they design on their computer.

It usually is.

For some reason I always knew of Bruno as the coding teacher so it still throws me when he starts talking about typography (not that he isn’t qualified).

I think of myself as a type and design teacher that also teaches code.
