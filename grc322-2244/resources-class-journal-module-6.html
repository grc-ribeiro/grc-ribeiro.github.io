“Don’t make it perfect just get it done.”

Yes. But now that you got it done, go back and make it great. (Not perfect, but the best you can.)

Designing with nothing on the page takes a long time.

And it doesn’t take you anywhere. Dump all the content on the artboard and make it work. Once you have something, you can improve it.

Do you have any advice for when one reaches a creative block? Or is feeling overwhelmed by the possibilities of laying out elements for our posters.

First of all, stop. Go do something else. Also, look for inspiration. Start by copying someone else’s work and then make it your own.

I always want to start with the design but I know I should start with content first.

With no content, there’s nothing to be designed.

I found that treating the content like rectangular blocks and roughly arranging them into a layout before adjusting the finer details of spacing and alignment was helpful! This way, you don’t lose sight of the overall composition when you zoom in later.

That’s one way to do it for sure.

It’s difficult to understand how big all of our type is going to be at actual size. I always try to do ⌘1 to see what it would look like at real size, but my computer isn’t big enough to be able to fully understand the size.

I’d never be able to make these decisions without printing out a tiled version of the poster and taping it together on the wall.

Poster takes so long for me and I’m curious how long it would take a professional graphic designer.

I don’t think anyone would design a poster like that too quickly. It’s a complex task.

Not only that. Keep in mind that you will go back to the group to design the final posters with them. But you’ll have more ideas.
