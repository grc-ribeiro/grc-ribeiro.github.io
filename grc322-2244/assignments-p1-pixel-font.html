Pixel font

The block numbers in the example above are functional, but lazy. The set is too mechanical. The letterforms in the example below are more organic and they would probably harmonize better with upper and lowercase letters of a typeface.


In this case, the second set compromises a little on legibility to increase the readability. In other words, the numbers on the second set are not as easily recognizable individually, but are more enjoyable as a set. (Especially in light mode. Tasks

Download this template and place your font on the guides.

Design 10 characters of a typeface (H, a, n, d, g, l, o, v, e, s) and represent them as 9-square tall with black and white only.

Each square must have one and only one color. Each square is either all black or all white. Breaking the squares in subparts is not “thinking outside the box”, it’s cheating.

Also, in case it’s not clear, the H is the only uppercase letter. The others are all lowercase and should have the proper ascenders and descenders, when applicable. But ascenders and descenders all need to fit in the same 9-square-tall grid.

Tip

Don’t spend hours perfecting the first letters before you work on the others.

Instead, take five minutes to quickly have all the letters there. Then, spend as many hours as you want or need to make them the best you can.

Rubric

  • Legibility

    40 points

    Each character is easily recognizable for what it’s supposed to be.

    • 40 pts Legible
    • 30 pts Almost legible
    • 20 pts Partially legible
    • 0 pts Illegible or non-existent

  • Readability

    40 points

    The set is enjoyable to read. The letters flow well and have personality even with such limitations, but the shapes are not distracting. In small sizes, they even look like a normal font (non-pixel).

    • 40 pts The Goldilocks of readability
    • 30 pts Too quirky
    • 20 pts Too mechanic
    • 0 pts Incomplete or non‑existent

  • Consistency

    20 points

    The letterform of each character is consistent with the set.

    • 20 pts Very consistent
    • 15 pts Almost consistent
    • 10 pts Partially consistent
    • 0 pts Very inconsistent or non‑existent

Submission

Submit it to Canvas as a PDF file, using the name convention lastname-pixel-font.pdf. (Replace lastname with your actual last name, please.)

Please check Canvas for deadline.
