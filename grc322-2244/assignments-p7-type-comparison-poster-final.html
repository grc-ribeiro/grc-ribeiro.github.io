GRC 322: Assignments

Type comparison (final posters)

As promissed in the first round, now you go back to your group to work on one final poster for the group.

Tasks

  1. Read the feedback from the first round.

  2. Get rid of the big ampersands, they’re so 2011. They’re not as cool as you think, they’re just a cliché at this point.

  3. Watch this short video on important typographic details.

  4. Meet with your group, but leave your ego outside.

  5. Share your files and feedback with your group. Once again, it’s important not to be defensive of your ideas. You’re working collectively to find the best solution.

  6. During the meeting, don’t open InDesign. Discuss the solutions verbally or through rough sketches.

  7. Individually, work on the idea the group decided that was the best to go with.

  8. Go back to the group to decide on the best final execution and polish the details. (You probably want to work on one InDesign file now.)

Requirements

Same as round one. Except it’s only one poster now.

  1. Format

    24″ wide
    36″ tall
    Horizontal posters are an automatic zero.

  2. History of each typeface

    For each typeface, include facts such as who designed it, when, and where. Also, explain if there was a specific purpose for its design and the historical context of its creation.

  3. Comparison

    Explain, through images and diagrams, what are the biggest similarities and big differences between the two typefaces assigned to your group.

  4. Style

    For the main text use a typeface that is very different from both typefaces you are researching. Suggestion: if your typefaces are sans-serif, use a serif typeface for your copy; and vice-versa.

(Don’t include the visual references.)

Style tips

Same as round one.

  1. Just as on the webpage, make sure your lines don’t get longer than 60 characters.

  2. Turn-off hyphenation.

  3. Check for widows.

  4. Check your rags carefully.

  5. You don’t want indentation on your poster. It’s always either indentation or skipping a line. On a book, you use indentation; on a poster, you skip a line. But never both.

  6. Check your hyphens and dashes.

  7. Don’t use default fonts. You probably have access to Adobe Fonts, use it.

Evaluation

  • History

    10 points

    Are the facts context for each typeface properly described and explained on the page? Did you include type designer, year, and location? Did you try to set political and artistic context in which the typeface was designed and released?

    • 10 pts Complete
    • 8 pts Partially complete
    • 5 pts Incomplete
    • 0 pts No information

  • Comparison

    30 points

    Did you do a thorough comparison of the typefaces, including uppercase, lowercase, punctuation, and numerals? Is the comparison effective? Would someone be able to distinguish them after reading your poster? (Somewhere between twelve or twenty images of comparison at least.) Thorough comparison, including uppercase, lowercase, punctuation, and numerals. One would be able to easily distinguish both typefaces after visiting the page.

    • 30 pts Complete
    • 20 pts Partially complete
    • 10 pts Incomplete
    • 0 pts No information

  • Typography

    30 points

    Are the typefaces appropriate? Does the visual hierarchy reflect the content hierarchy? Do the paragraph lines have an appropriate length? Is the type size adequate for legibility? Is the leading adequate for the type size?

    • 30 pts Satisfactory
    • 25 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 15 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Grid

    20 points

    Are all the elements properly aligned to the grid? Is the grid being well used to convey planning, intelligibility, clarity, orderliness, and credibility? Are elements very well aligned within the grid making the layouts organized but also visually interesting? At the same time, does it show enough flexibility of the grid?

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Color and Consistency

    10 points

    Is color being used effectively to direct users to attract attention, group elements, indicate meaning, and enhance aesthetics? Are similar parts related? Are related parts similar? Are the variations consistently applied in all parts of the poster?

    • 10 pts Satisfactory
    • 8 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 5 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

Submission

  1. Export your poster twice, once showing the grid, and once without it.

  2. Name the file without the grid using the name convention lastname-lastname-lastname-type-poster-2-grid-hidden.pdf.

  3. Name the file with the grid using the name convention lastname-lastname-lastname-type-poster-2-grid-show.pdf.

  4. Submit both files to Canvas.

Please check Canvas for deadline.

(One submission per groups. No design decision explanation necessary.)
