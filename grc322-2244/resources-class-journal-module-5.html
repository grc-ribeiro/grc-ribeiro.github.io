GRC 322: Resources

Class journal, module 5

A bag of chips is not enough to keep you going on all day. Eat before class starts so you’re not delirious the entire time.

Please do.

It’s rewarding to be able to use skills from 338 and 318 to come together and create a 322 project … the connection between all my classes makes learning feel like it has a purpose which didn’t happen in high school.

And it’s not an accident.

After looking closely at the typeface comparisons it was more difficult to find similarities than differences.

That’s interesting.

At the beginning of class, my group struggled with finding differences between our two typefaces. By the end of class, we were struggling to find similarities.

Mission accomplished.

I learned that the typeface Futura has been very successful and widely used—it was even on the plaque that Buzz Aldrin and Neil Armstrong left as the first people to set foot on the moon!

Hence, the picture of the moon on the type comparison poster that is featured outside the lab.

It was fun to combine 338/339 topics with the assignment! A nice reminder that making an effective webpage can be easy.

And effective.

It can be tricky to work collaboratively on design assignments in terms of file sharing and keeping everything consistent.

That’s your career from now on, though. This is the job. Design is work.

I like the idea of working in groups on design projects because I feel like it is good practice for working with a team at a company.

Exactly.

Before GrC, I never would have noticed the more technical, minute aspects of typography and font design. Now I love looking for cool typography in my daily life.

It’s a curse.

Don’t forget alt text when including images on a webpage!

Very important indeed!

I think our group will be able to get more creative with our stylesheet next week to add a bit of flair to the project.

Nope. That’s it. The page was only informational. The real project is the poster that comes next.

I think that making a font set would be a cool portfolio piece!

A tough one, though. You may want to design a logo with custom characters instead?
