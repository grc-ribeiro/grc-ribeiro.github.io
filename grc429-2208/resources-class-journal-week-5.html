Class journal, week 5

I think this is by far the most applicable class to our concentration, and I really enjoy it.

I said in the beginning that this is the class that gets students jobs.

How would this project benefit us in the future?

I’ll go about it in details in the last week of class. But here’s an email I got this last summer:

Hi Bruno,
 
This happened a while ago but I wanted to send you a note so you can brag to future classes.
 
I had a final interview for the company that you helped me with the design challenge and 429 felt like a cheat sheet for it. Every question they asked had been pretty directly addressed in class. It felt great to light them up with very confident answers.
 
And that’s where I’ll be working after graduation, so thank you!

Bad design can make users feel dumb and incapable.

Yes. It’s mean actually.

Im confused on if we are supposed to focus on wireframing and showing what the app that pairs with the AR glasses will look like or what the view will look like through the AR glasses.

For some of you, the main interaction will be on the phone, not on the glass, so it’s ok to use the phone instead for wireframe and testing. But the AR needs to happen on the glass.

I’m confused why we can’t do wireframing through digital graphics instead of handrawing them.

You do wireframes in the computer. That’s this week. But the preliminary sketches are done by hand to go faster. With hand sketches, especially in small thumbnails, you can’t get caught up in the details that not relevant now. (They’ll be relevant later, though.)

It is always fun to draw horrible stick figures and have your teacher say they look great!

If they did the job, they did the job.

One of the things I have been trying to do each week is really improve based on your feedback. Are we encouraged to continue to make changes to old things as the project continues to move forward?

Always.

You should limit the amount of options a user can view at one time, but not so limited that they find it difficult or irritating to navigate.

To design is to make decisions. How many is too many and how few is too few?

Next week is wireframes. I looked at the assignment, and it says actual content. My understanding of wireframes was usually it’s just a sketch for layout purposes.

Each person and team have their own process. I don’t see the point of designing wireframes with fake content. Wireframes should convey more than just a rough idea of what the layout would be. The reason we do it digitally is to sweat details in size, position, and hierarchy. And you can’t work on them without the real content.

The scenarios were really nice for getting everyone on the same page as well as helping us come up with important new factors to keep in mind for when we design the screens.

Yes, scenarios and interaction framework are great for ensuring that everybody is on the same page, which is often assumed but not true.

I learned that route monitoring is helpful to make sure that a user is heading in the right direction.

Often overlooked part of wayfinding.

It’s interesting to see all of the laws and theories that we learned in the past when designing in 338 to be used in this class too!

Both dealing with digital interactive products.

This project has honestly made me feel more innovative. I feel more aware of problems I have now and how they could be fixed.

Awesome! Looking forward to hearing about your next adventures!
