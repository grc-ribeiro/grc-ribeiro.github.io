Class journal, week 9

This week was pretty stressful for me, and I just want to say that I really appreciate how you accommodate students’ wellbeing as well as busy schedules!

I’m always happy to accommodate. I don’t want my class to be reason for stress.

I really enjoy how this class was working on a project the whole time. I never felt too overwhelmed but that I was learning a lot!

It’s surprising smooth, isn’t it?

I was really nervous coming into this class because I didn’t know how to do any of the usability testing, wire-framing, prototyping, etc. I remember marking everything as “not sure” in the initial survey, but when I retook the exit survey, I was glad that I knew how to do basically everything. Working with a partner and going step by step each week helped me learn a lot!

It’s almost like I know what I’m doing.

Adobe XD, Figma, and Sketch seem to have similar functions when designing for UX/UI, but when would you suggest using one platform over another? Are there specific benefits and disadvantages for each?

Beside personal (or team) preference, Figma is the best for real-time collaboration. Sketch is promising to offer it soon, though.

Do you have any ideas or resources for how to create a strong AR goggle mockup?

You can try Spark AR Studio and Adobe Aero

I do wish there was a GrC class that taught us more about Adobe XD.

Adobe XD and similar tools are so simple and easy to use that you can just start exploring it. Google if you need help. It’s not worth teaching in a class, because it’s richer if you keep learning as you come up with other uses.

Having to write about technical requirements and possible constraints has made me think about real products and what type of technology they use, and it’s not something I’ve thought about very frequently.

It’s good that you have it in your report now, before someone asks you about them in a future interview.

I think feature creep is a really hard thing to avoid in the context of an application. There’s so much potential that runs through your head that it’s hard to avoid coming to the realization that there’s always more to be done. I’m glad that we got to write those thoughts down as potential improvements for the future.

Absolutely, it’s there to avoid feature creep. Always helpful in any project: “That’s a good idea! Let’s put on the roadmap and not worry about it now.”

When understanding what possible constraints your product has, it is best to think of scenarios that could be of inconvenience, such as the product not working correctly, or scenarios where your product could be hazardous.

That’s a very good way to explain it. I may use it in the future.

Congratulations! Thanks for sharing the good news!
