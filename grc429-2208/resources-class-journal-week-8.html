GRC 429: Resources

Class journal, week 8

I’m worried about this project/case study because it’s so conceptual, not sure if employers will even like this type of work.

I’ve said it many times, this is the project that gives my students a job. It’s not conceptual at all. The process is all there. And employers will appreciate not seeing at yet another recipe app for the iPhone.

I know you mentioned that people get jobs because of the project in this class. Do you think people put the entire report in their portfolios or just talk about the project/process in interviews?

They don’t put the whole report in their portfolios. They show the project and some process and they talk about it in interviews. This project get students jobs, after they got an interview.

Bouncing off that last point, this project hasn’t been a huge stresser in my school life. Sometimes final projects can be daunting especially if they aren’t started in advance, so the structure of this project has really helped.

I always tell my students that this is the least class they will take with me. They never believe in the beginning.

I liked that we were separated from our group members for the ethical considerations assignment because it gave us all a chance to come up with ideas totally independently and get many people’s feedback, so we were able to combine so many good ideas that no one person would have thought of on their own.

That was the idea. I’m really glad it worked well.

Will discussions be incorporated into the next few steps of our project? I found the feedback from people to be pretty useful.

I’m glad it worked so well. Honestly, I wasn’t so sure. But that’s it, we’re almost done here.

A few people in last week’s journals mentioned how great Adobe XD is. I was introduced to it in a class over the summer and loved it. It’s so sleek and easy to figure out without much of a learning curve, and the prototyping aspect is fun. :D

I find all those tools (Adobe XD, Figma, Sketch, etc.) quite intuitive and super fun to make interactive mock‑ups.

For the nice image to go with our abstract, does it have to be an image we create?

Yes. It’s an image of your product and its interface.

I love being able to see the progress our group makes every week through the report updates. The report updates each week has made the project very manageable!

So many of you wrote similar thoughts this week. I’m glad you’re all enjoying the class.

I liked working individually, and then meeting with my group later in the week to hear their ideas. It gave me time to really consider our project. My group mates thought of ideas I never would have considered.

Best way to collaborate. It’s almost like you are also enrolled in GRC 322.

Going from the wireframes to the high definition layouts made a huge difference. Usability analysis testing the wireframes was a good idea because it’s not set in stone yet.

And they communicate it well too.

Color schemes are harder to decide on than I thought.

They’re hard. Between only you and me, it’s my least favorite part of any design project.

For my project, my group looked more into Apple’s app specifications and made our layout to be more compliant with their specifications. One big takeaway that we had was we made edits to our icons to make them look more like what Apple uses. We hope this will create a sense of familiarity with those who use our app.

Sounds like a good strategy

I have done a few GRC projects about product and app development, and I have never been tasked to do ethical considerations until now.

I’m glad you did, then.

I really enjoyed thinking about the ethics of our product this week, it makes you think about how many other companies ignored ethics when making decisions about how their products would affect the environment or people’s health.

Sadly, true.

Is there any one ethical approach we should focus on within the five sources of ethical standards? Or are they all correct in one way or another.

They are all valid lens to look through. And more often than not, they lead to the same conclusion.

Just wondering, but at the end of the project could we have a discussion board where people post some of their high fidelity mockups or images? I’m just super curious as to what everyone decided to do since the two projects I read about in my discussion board were so creative and cool.

Good idea! Let’s do this. I usually have a final presentation in class. But for this year, a discussion board seems to be the best option.

This isn’t entirely on you but since my other projects in GrC are also UI concepts, I’m getting so burnt out on working in Figma. Have any advice for dealing with burnout when it comes to design work?

Go out for some physical activity. Swimming, running, and soccer are my preferred workouts, but you choose the one that relaxes you the most.

This is somewhat irrelevant, but the political anxiety surrounding the election definitely made me think of the effects of politics on various aspects of life, including business and travel–something that my product is affected by.

Not irrelevant at all. Politics affect everything. I believe you mention political anxiety because of the presidential election. But please always do your homework on local elections too, including all the propositions. (Those seem to be the hardest to decide on how to vote, but your vote counts, and I know your generation really cares. Better than your generation, only the ones that will come next!)
