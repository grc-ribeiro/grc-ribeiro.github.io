Interviews weren’t as helpful as I expected, at least for me, the people I interviewed were fine with the way things are right now, and they confused AR and VR which is understandable. After the interview, when I told one participant our project, he had lots of interesting ideas that we hadn’t even thought of, which gave me more insight than the actual interviews. I’m looking forward to gathering all of our interviews and data to see what trends we noticed and how much they will change our final design.

If you ask people what they want, they’ll say faster horses. The interview is not to give you ideas for the product. You want to understand their process, then you can notice opportunities for improvement, even if they themselves can’t see how things can be improved. It’s not their job to design new processes and products to help them.

The interviews were good but I feel like it was hindered a bit because of COVID.

I get it.

Were the interviews supposed to be people that closely fit our persona or could they have variations like gender?

You interview whomever you find that could be interested in your projects. Your persona then is based on the interviews, not the other way around.

When we did our interviews this week, we learned a good strategy- one person would ask the questions, one would keep track of which questions we should ask next, and one of us wrote down their answers. It went smoothly, though I did learn that more questions pop up than what we had planned.

It does sound like a good strategy.

Finally, I think my biggest takeaway from this week’s content was a quote by Alan Cooper, which states that “the persona is a vessel for your learnings interviewing real people", further reemphasizing that the persona is not just some random person that we let our imaginations run wild creating, but rather somebody reflective of exhaustive research and the needs, desires, and wants of real people.

There’s way more analysis than imagination here.

I think that having a quote for the persona is a great way of getting in their head and figuring out their wants/needs.

I didn’t use quotes when I first start using personas back in grad school. I only learned about them when I taught for Alan Cooper’s consultancy back in 2015. I thought it was really helpful too.

Is Seth based off of a real person you know? I feel like you had very real and strong feelings against that man (I get it).

No, not really. It was just a joke.

Just curious, why is it so important how precisely we name the personas and choose a photo for them? Typically I just use a random name generator to get some options of fairly generic but plausible names. The resource thispersondoesnotexist.com was really helpful for photos, though.

Random name generator is not a bad idea. You just want your persona to be believable, but not too generic that sounds fake or too unique to be a distraction.

My mom works in the entertainment industry, and part of her job is researching names to make sure that the  film/tv company is using a generic enough name that they won’t get sued for using someone’s actual name. It can be really difficult for the tv/film industry to think of names that are believable enough, but also are not too specific to one person or buisness(especially if the show/film takes place in a large city). This week’s lesson of personas reminded me of my mom’s job because, unless she is working on a cartoon, she also cannot do alteration for character names. Just like the personas we created this week, my mom also needs to find a balance of real and generic.

That’s awesome! Never thought about that part of research in the entertainment industry. I’ll share this information with my students in the future.

The lecture was very informative as to how specific the persona should be. I think I can be too vague sometimes, so that reminded me to include lots of details.

Not too many, though.

It is not supposed to be easy. If you found it difficult but were happy with the end result, I’ll assume feel like you’ve learned something.

Also, the pictures in Unsplash are too nicely done. Doesn’t seem real people in real life. Which is nice in some context, but not for personas.
