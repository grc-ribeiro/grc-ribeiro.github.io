GRC 429: Resources

Class journal, week 1

I enjoyed reading this week’s article, Why is so much of design school a waste of time? by Juliette Cezzar. I can sometimes have a hard time receiving feedback on my designs so it was helpful to have some insight about how to use feedback to your advantage.  I will hold onto her advice to “Ask real questions that tell you something that you didn’t know already. Say ‘tell me more.’ Let them wander, tell stories, not understand, be irrelevant — take as long as it takes to listen for the pieces that make you better”.

When Professor Cezzar published this article, I promised her on Twitter that I’d share with my students on the first day of every class that I teach.

The article “Why is so much of design school a waste of time” reaffirmed something I already knew but haven’t fully accepted yet. While I understand the importance of criticism, sometimes it can be hard for me to know how to respond to it. My initial response would be to defend my reasoning for the design choice in question, but I know that it can come off as sensitive and ignorant. I genuinely would rather hear some sort of correction than to get no feedback at all, which I think is a good start. This article reminded me of my first internship when I was a second year, and was asked to redesign the startup company’s website. The guidelines were very loose, and when I produced my interpretation of said guidelines, I was met with a “that’s not quite what I’m looking for”. At first I mentally blamed it on the lack of clear instruction, but as I progressed later into my education and work experience, I learned that designers are expected to work with loose guidelines and that rework is normal and okay. I really liked this article because it reminded me that it is okay to be told to make changes. I feel weird when someone accepts my work as is anyway.

Great points about loose guidelines and rework.
