Every week, you will work on the same assignment, which is a report that will be updated with new content as the project evolves. Please, download the report template and follow the instructions for each week.

Report update (week 2)

On week 2, this is what is expected on your report:

  1. Cover
    Name of the institution, name of the department, number, and name of the class; (tentative) name of the project; one-line description; name of the students (in alphabetical order by last name); date. Don’t edit on the page itself. Use variables.

  2. Table of contents
    The table of contents will be automatically generated if you continue to use paragraph styles, which you should anyway. Go to Layout > Update Table of Content in InDesign before export your file to PDF.

  3. Schedule
    Make a schedule for your project. (It’s, of course, based on the schedule for the class, but they’re not the same thing. Check Canvas for examples of good schedules from previous years.)

  4. Abstract (tentative)
    Write one or two paragraphs about your product. (It’s about the product, not about your process.) Try to answer these questions: What does it do? How does it do it? Why is it different? Write in present tense. (Check Canvas for examples of good abstracts from previous years.)

  5. Audience
    Explain who you are designing for. If your audience is too broad, try to narrow it, by explaining the context on what where you envision your product being used and the problems you anticipate solving.

 

Submit your PDF to Canvas, by Thursday, 24 September 2020, at 11:59pm, using the name convention lastname1-lastname2-w2.pdf. Use alphabetical order by last name. And mind the spelling of all names.
Always submit the whole document as it is now. One submission per group is sufficient.
Good typography will be always required and expected in this class.

Report update (week 3)

On week 3, this is what is expected on your report:

  1. Competitive landscape
    On a professional, “real life” experience, the competitive analysis can take several weeks and address issues such as business model, that are not a concern for our academic experience in this course. Furthermore, since your product is innovative, it may not have a direct competitor. It does have, however, a competitive landscape where it will live. Search for images of user interfaces in similar or related products. You may also include physical products that your target audience currently use to accomplish the same goals or perform similar tasks. Also, include images of physical and digital products that share the same domain (e.g. hospital, teaching, driving, sorting, rescuing, etc). Make sure to include a caption for all your images.

  2. Research questions
    Explain the objectives of the interview and the questions you will ask.

Report update (week 4)

On week 4, this is what is expected on your report:

  1. Summary of the answers
    For each participant of the ethnographic interview, list the main findings. You may write in paragraphs or bullet points. (You don’t need to include the full transcript of each interview.)

  2. Persona
    Introduce your persona, with a name, age, and a picture. Describe, in a paragraph, information about their lives that are relevant to your project. Include a quote to summarize their main goal. One persona per project only.

  3. Goals
    List what your persona would accomplish with your product. Don’t list your goals for the project. It’s the persona’s goals.

Report update (week 5)

On week 5, this is what is expected on your report:

  1. Scenario
    Write a story where your persona interacts with your product. It’s not a story where someone buys the product. They already have it. It should be a typical use case, but it should also include a few interactions with the product. Use your sketches with (typed) captions. The sketches don’t need to be high-skilled pieces of art, but make them neat in Photoshop. (At very least, use the Levels tool to make the background white.)

  2. Interaction framework
    Sketches of the user interface of your product. Draw the major screens for the keypath scenario. Number each step. Again, make them neat in Photoshop. (At very least, use the Levels tool to make the background white.)

Report update (week 6)

On week 6, this is what is expected on your report:

  1. Wireframes
    Include the wireframes for keypath scenarios and some important validation scenarios for your product.

    Actual content: No placeholder or filling.

    Establish visual hierarchy: Remember proximity, alignment, visibility, etc.

    No color palette: Only green or red if semantically necessary.

    No typeface selection concerns: Use Helvetica, Calibri, or Roboto.

    Placeholder for images: No final images, only rough sketches.

    Check for inconsistency: Sizes, position, language, functionality, etc.

    You can use any tool that you want, but I suggest Adobe XD since your next step is to make it interactive.

  2. Usability analysis tests
    Explain what tasks will be given to your user in the task analysis test.

Report update (week 10)

On week 10, this is what is expected on your report:

  1. Design principles analysis
    Write a brief paragraph (one to three short sentences) for each design principle, explaining how they informed the design decisions you made during the design process.

  2. Final product
    Include your final layouts for the screens on your application. You don’t need every single screen of your product, but it needs to be comprehensive enough to explain the main features and interactions. Use captions and annotations to explain how the product works.

  3. A nice image to go with the abstract on page 3 of your report.
    It can be an image of the most significant screen of your product, or maybe an image of someone using it. You want it to be the image that will communicate the best what your product does. (See the examples of abstract on Canvas.)

  4. Anything else that is missing
    At this point, your report should be really close to done. It’s the first draft of your final report. If there’s anything that’s missing, please include it too.
