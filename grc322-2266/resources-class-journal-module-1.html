GRC 322: Resources

Class journal, module 1

I should reach out for help and not assume when I find things confusing.

That’s very important in an online class. But also applies to anything in life?

I learnt that making a pixel font is really hard. I’m nervous to do the 6 pixel version, the 9 was already difficult to make the letters look like they are supposed to, but add character.

The 9-pixel font is the easiest by far. That’s why we start with it.

Pixel fonts are much more complex to make than I thought.

I know, right? Not as simple as it seems.

Pixel fonts may not be as important in today’s digital with the invention of high-resolution screens, however, they offer a great learning opportunity to see the details in letterforms when designing typefaces.

Exactly.

I didn’t realize how many different types of classifications there are and how similar some look. I hope to get better at identifying them.

You will.

I learned that Didone typefaces look elegant but aren’t good for long blocks of text. I hope to gain a better understanding of when to use different typefaces.

You will.

Something I want to investigate next is designing typefaces in vector form.

That’s two years in grad school and a lifetime of suffering over minutiae.

I am excited to work on the designer poster project! Researching all of the designers listed was very inspirational, and it was really cool to see how greatly the styles of their work differed, as well as the different ways they used unique typography alongside graphics.

The next step of the assignment is published with the assigned designers.
