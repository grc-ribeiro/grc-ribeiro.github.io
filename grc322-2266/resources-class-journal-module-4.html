I am getting lots of practice with poster layout.

Yes, you are!

I always forget to set the baseline grid you have to go into the Preferences section of InDesign!

While you are there, uncheck the Grids in the back option.

It was fun to pick out different color combinations for the poster this week. I like using coolors.co when I need inspiration for color palettes.

I highly recommend it. Once you’re done with your layouts, it doesn’t take much to just test the same layout in different color combinations.

I learned the best practice for moving a word to the next line is to use a hard space to connect with the next word.

It is.

On a poster, lines should be on the shorter side, between 40 and 50 characters per line.

True. On books it should be longer, though.

Type on a poster should not be centered, but rather aligned to your grid.

Just stop centering your type. When it doesn’t look cheap, it still looks lazy.
