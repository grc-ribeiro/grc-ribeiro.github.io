GRC 322: Resources

Class journal, module 6

It is important to listen to your body and take breaks.

Yes!

Because I’m so used to designing for 8.5x11” (or smaller), it feels foreign to be designing for such a large canvas. I keep trying to remind myself about the different distances someone might read this poster from.

It really is different.

I am not too thrilled with my research posters. Graphic design really is not my thing.

That’s not true.

I never really thought about the specifics of designing posters of a certain size such as the size of your body text, heading, etc.

Now you did. I was in my junior year in college when one of my favorite professors in college saw some of us designing a poster for another class. He told us: “Do you know how to design a poster? It’s not only enlarging a book cover.” It stuck with me.

I think I was more stressed for this project than I had to be.

If you were stressed at all, then yes, you were more stressed than you had to be.

Making multiple good looking posters is hard!

I know. That’s what you signed up for, though. And I know you’re up to the task.

This was one of my favorite projects in GrC. Quick, intensive, intriguing, and with a lot of important design decisions to make. Also, lots of freedom to design. I enjoyed it a lot.

You have no idea how much it warms my heart. It is one my favorite projects among all the classes I’ve taught.

When you design a big poster you don’t just scale a smaller poster/design. For a big poster there are 3 levels of reading (10 ft, 3 ft, and 1 ft).

Exactly. Those three numbers are arbitrary, though. I think I’ve made them up. But that’s still how you should think about the poster.

After working on this in class I had mental block so I changed scenery and was very productive at home!

Sometimes it helps. You do miss the collaboration, but sometimes you need to move away from a windowless computer lab in an old concrete building.

I learned working on a lab assignment makes the train ride go by a lot faster.

You’re welcome. Let me know if you need extra assignment for your ride back.

It was sad not getting to sit next to my group members.

You get to make new friends, though!

I didn’t use transparency in my design but it was useful to learn about screen and multiply. I want to use that in the future.

Years ago, watching students work in this assignment, I realized that this knowledge was lacking. It’s quite helpful to know the modes.

Transparency modes were very fun to play with. Instead of using screen for transparency on a dark background, I used multiply for a highlight color atop of a white letterform. It highlights the area on the letterform and slightly tints the background with the shape of the highlight.

Now you know what the modes are and how to use them.

I unexpectedly liked the second poster I made much more than my first, which I was certain was my baby.

The third might be even better.

I want to try more layouts because I don’t love the ones I made, but they will have to do for now. The grid was helpful as usual.

Maybe coming with fresh eyes will help you.

It’s kind of intimidating starting with a blank page but I just took a deep breath and started by creating a grid.

Never stare at the blank page. Dump content on it and make it look good.

This week, I really struggled to find inspiration for my posters. While I like working with a grid, sometimes I feel really restricted and it blocks my creative process. I’m not super happy with what I’ve turned in, but I think what I’ve designed is a good start.

If you don’t like working with the grid, start without the grid and add it later to tight things up.

This week’s assignment gave me a better understanding of how to experiment with color and text placements within different grids.

I’m glad to hear it’s working.
