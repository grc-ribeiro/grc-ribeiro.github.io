GRC 322: Resources

Class journal, module 4

I felt really out of it for some reason today. Maybe it’s because it was a Monday.

It happens. I wasn’t feeling too hot on Wednesday, for example. Couldn’t even explain or understand why. But towards the end of the day (and I promise I’m not making this up), when I started working on the poster critique video, my mood got better. But we all have our bad days.

Although the poster design is stressful, I’m learning a lot.

It shouldn’t be stressful. Challenging, but not stressful. Seriously, don’t stress about it. Put it in perspective; what’s the worst that can happen?

I loved looking into my designer this week, Milton Glaser. I had never heard of him before but he has such an iconic style that is incredibly famous. He made the “I ❤️ NY” design which truly speaks for itself! Cool assignment to have us look into impactful designers.

Let’s print them out and hang them where everyone can learn too.

I also learned that I over complicate things and stress myself out for no reason. That probably won’t ever change, but it’s at least something I can acknowledge. Knowing that about myself, I think it’s important to take breaks. Sometimes simply stepping away from a design and coming back to it later with fresh eyes is the key to success.

Everybody should take breaks often.

I enjoyed the challenge of using the multiple grids. I created layouts I never had before—very outside my comfort zone, in a good way.

That’s the idea!

I know you gave us a fake time limit to scare us, but getting all my basic layouts out quickly without thinking too much was very nice and made edits easier. I might try it again sometime.

Yes, try that more often. Get some quick (and bad?) layouts done quickly. You have no option but improving it then.

I want to see more posters for reference next time because sometimes my creativity is just not there.

Always look for references.

It was interesting to try a bunch of different ideas all at once because I feel like my design process is usually more linear, following one idea all the way through before trying something else. I think I do this because I have a vision and I want to see my full vision played out before judging it, but I can see the value in doing rough layouts like this to just get a sense of how it could look. This method of creating a bunch of different layouts all at once challenged my perfectionist tendencies and forced me to be okay with “good enough” so that I could get to all the layouts within the lab time (I still only finished 4 layouts during the lab though because of who I am as a person).

To design is to make decisions. You need to try different possibilities before deciding on what works best.

This was a really big assignment and I learned that designing layouts is super time consuming!

It should be. But you all need to get better at making quick layouts to test ideas.

Chase Iseley was very helpful and answered my questions when I couldn’t make it to lab!

Thanks for helping, Chase!

I didn’t realize how many fonts were designed based on other fonts… it seems like almost all of them. My designer’s most popular typefaces were based on Baskerville and Bodoni.

“Nothing is lost, nothing is created, everything is transformed.”

I enjoyed learning about Ruth Ansel! She is a fascinating woman who broke barriers in the world of magazine publishing.

She is and she did.

Looking at so many old movie posters made me realize that most movie posters today are terrible and lack character. :(

You’re not wrong. The are all the same orange and blue trope.

My roommate and I think that it’s a little funny that Saul Bass is famous for designing graphics for so many horror movies but also designed the Girl Scouts logo.

That’s an interesting poing.

Once you align your texts and photos to the grid, it’s very satisfying to look at.

Yes. Once the grid clicks, it all makes a lot of sense.

My typeface designer co-founded this type foundry and it’s really cool: https://www.emigre.com/.

I subscribe to their font catalogs (for free!). You can also download their free PDF type specimens.

I’m good with columns but the baseline grid is not fun.

You’re wrong. Baseline grids are awesome.

Pay attention to line length (different for poster vs. book, etc.).

Yes. Good point, often overlooked.

I only like a few of my designer posters. Some of them are just ugly and I greatly wish to throw them out.

You will. But we have to try different possibilities.

I love creative movie title sequences so I was thrilled to learn that my designer revolutionized title sequences. Saul Bass is so cool.

I miss teaching motion graphics and movie title sequences.

New readings on the class website.
