GRC 322: Assignments

Pixel font (final)

For this assignment, you will adapt the same font you designed in module 1 for two more sizes: 6‑square height and 12‑square height.

Please use the same template you used last week.

You want your three representations of the same font.

You will need, however, to make compromises on the 6‑square font. On the 12‑square height, though, you won’t need some of the compromises you did on the 9‑square font.

Rubric for each set

  • Legibility

    10 points

    Each character is easily recognizable for what it’s supposed to be.

    • 10 pts Legible
    • 8 pts Almost legible
    • 5 pts Partially legible
    • 0 pts Illegible or non-existent

  • Readability

    10 points

    The set is enjoyable to read. The letters flow well and have personality even with such limitations, but the shapes are not distracting. In small sizes, they even look like a normal font (non-pixel).

    • 10 pts The Goldilocks of readability
    • 8 pts Too quirky
    • 5 pts Too mechanic
    • 0 pts Incomplete or non‑existent

  • Consistency

    5 points

    The letterform of each character is consistent with the set.

    • 5 pts Very consistent
    • 3 pts Almost consistent
    • 2 pts Partially consistent
    • 0 pts Very inconsistent or non‑existent

Rubric for the family

  • Consistency

    25 points

    The letterforms of each set is consistent with the other sets in the family.

    • 25 pts Very consistent
    • 20 pts Almost consistent
    • 10 pts Partially consistent
    • 0 pts Very inconsistent or non‑existent
