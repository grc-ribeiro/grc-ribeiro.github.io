Class journal, week 5

How is it already week 6????

“Time flies like an arrow. And fruit flies like a banana.”

Were there no highlights from last week? I did not realize I never read week 4 highlights until I was writing this week’s journal.

Of course there were! I read all entries, selected some, and did write my comments. I only forgot to make them available to you. Thanks for pointing that out!

On a related note, I really enjoy writing these journals since they make reflect on what I learned and what stood out the most to me this week from all the new information that was thrown at me.

I’ve always enjoyed reading them. Even more so now that I can’t chat with you all in the lab (while you would be trying to concentrate on your code).

Even through this has been the largest project so far, I am struggling the least. The past weeks have prepared me well.

That was the plan.

I feel like the progression from week to week is really helping me remember the material and how to code.

But don’t forget that you can go back to the videos, to your notes, to other resources. You don’t need to remember everything.

I am still a little bit confused on lists in HTML, and the appropriate ways to use them. I understand the basic functions of ul/ol, but it gets a bit complicated for me when the lists get more complicated.

Let me know if you want any specific help. But the page on different uses of list tends to help.

I really wanted to use negative margins/padding even though I know it’s wrong.

You really don’t need them, unless you’re actually overlapping elements.

I would like to investigate different ways other companies use the “float” option, and how they incorporate it into their website design. I found the float option useful, but a bit difficult to use.

We’ll work more on it next week.

Im not 100% sure when to use divs and what is appropriate.

Use divs to groups elements together in a section of the page.

This was a good refresher on how to add line breaks to paragraph tags in HTML!

Please avoid the break tag!

I feel like I 100% understand how HTML and CSS work together to create a website, however based on how my restaurant website looks right now I don’t feel confident that I would be able to make a very professional looking website.

Well, right now, it should look like a skeleton of a website.

I have to pay closer attention to the specific wording of the code in lecture videos. I often have to reference back at it when completing projects or the quizzes.

Nothing wrong with referencing back at it.

I learned that the internet is my friend and there are so many helpful resources online for when I can’t figure something out.

Always good to search for solutions on the internet. But don’t forget to check the list of mistakes to avoid.

That the period at the end of the alt attribute text tells screen readers to pause.

Which is a small thing we can do to do help someone who can’t see.

I’m probably going to spend a lot of time on Illustrator next week to make my restaurant’s logo.

Please, don’t. Keep it simple.

Im not sure if I mentioned last week, but learning to apply font from the Adobe Creative Cloud into the CSS felt very beneficial.

Yes, it is. And it’s quite straight‑forward. Also, the typefaces selection is much better than on Google Fonts.

I really liked learning about Horror Vacui because that’s a tendency that I often fall into. I like to fill up white space with “things", but taking this class and reading about this reminds me that white space could be good and could signify minimalism and prestige.

And remember that blank space in not necessarily white. A big area with solid color is blank space and, depending on the color, just as effective to convey higher‑end/expensive products.

I was unaware of the influence face-ism ratio had over designs, though I understood expressions and poses had obvious power over a design, I honestly thought that the proximity of the face was mostly aesthetic preference.

Many times it is. And we do without thinking what’s the effect it has on the viewer’s perception of the portrayed.

I’ve never really thought about how arbitrary icons aren’t direct representations of what they stand for.

It really is hard to miss it, because we just understand them. But as designers, if we’re going to design or use icons, we need to look at them more carefully.

Learning that there is a name to a concept I could never identify or exactly describe was cool! Now I can add horror-vacui to my vocabulary!

And one of the goals of learning the design principles is to help you explain your design choices.

Also glad that we don’t have to do those peer evaluations anymore because I did not see them doing much.

You never had to do them. But there’re still open. Nothing’s changed.

Learned (FINALLY) how to get 100% on the design principle example because my group communicated with you to confirm screenshots and to go over our paragraphs.

Please do.

Good to know. Although…

Choosing good fonts and layouts is still difficult, and I plan to get better by trying out more things while being as consistent and simple as possible.

Being consistent and simple are good strategies. Also, look for references.
