How do UX/ UI designers determine their final prototypes?

That’s a great question. And the best approach is to rely on tests with users and a set of criteria.

I also was slightly confused by what distinguishes iteration and prototypes in general. I see how they’re different but each one seems like the designer starting with something, testing it, and then building upon the idea.

There’s definitely overlapping, as it’ll be the case with several principles that we’ll visit in class. Iterations may or may not include different prototypes.

I am very excited to continue learning about the design principles. People uneducated on design feel that it is all personal opinion about whether a design is good or not. However, there are principles and reasonings to why a design is the way it is. I am a person always curious to find the why, and design principles are the concrete why’s to designs.

Exactly. Knowing the principles makes our work easier.

I really enjoyed reading about the concept of the aesthetic-usability effect. It’s something that I’ve always known in my head, maybe subconsciously, but never had the words and terminology to describe it.

It will happen. Many of the principles will be concepts that we kind of knew before, but it really helps to have a name for it.

In my group discussion board, we were reminded aesthetic affects the perception of usability more so than desirability.

Not exactly. What I meant was that the principle related to usability and that should be the focus of your example. But aesthetics may affect more desirability than usability, but it’s just not the subject of the principle.

That’s a very good point. I’ve mentioned several times how you need to plan your prototype for what you want to learn, but it’s also for what you want to show.
