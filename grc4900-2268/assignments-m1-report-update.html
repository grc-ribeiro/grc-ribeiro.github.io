GRC 4900: Assignments

Report update Module 1

Every week, you will work on the same assignment, which is a report that will be updated with new content as the project evolves. Please, download the report templateand follow the instructions for each week.

Tasks

On week 1, this is what is expected on your report:

  1. Cover

    Name of the institution, name of the department, number, and name of the class; (tentative) name of the project; one-line description; name of the students (in alphabetical order by last name); date. Don’t edit on the page itself. Use variables.

  2. Table of contents

    The table of contents will be automatically generated if you continue to use paragraph styles, which you should anyway. Go to Layout > Update Table of Content in InDesign before export your file to PDF.

  3. Schedule

    Make a schedule for your project. (It’s, of course, based on the schedule for the class, but they’re not the same thing. Check Canvas for examples of good schedules from previous years.)

  4. Abstract (tentative)

    Write one or two paragraphs about your product. (It’s about the product, not about your process.) Try to answer these questions: What does it do? How does it do it? Why is it different? Write in present tense and don’t write the questions that it’s answering, just include the answers for the questions in the paragraphs. (Check Canvas for examples of good abstracts from previous years.)

  5. Audience

    Explain who you are designing for. If your audience is too broad, try to narrow it, by explaining the context on what where you envision your product being used and the problems you anticipate solving. Keep in mind that audience doesn’t mean demographics. You want to include characteristics that are relevant to the project as users, such as their proficiency with the subject matter, current challenges, and the context in which they experience the problem. (A persona is not required at this stage.)

Rubric

  • Cover

    20 points

    Did you check if you have all the requirements for the cover?

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Table of contents

    20 points

    Did you update the TOC and it worked correctly? (If you didn’t use an automatic TOC, I guarantee you that it will show eventually.)

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Schedule

    20 points

    The schedule is specific enough for your needs? Did you compare with the schedule for the class? Also, did you check the examples on Canvas? (Keep in mind that those were in the quarter systems, you need one for the semester.)

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Abstract

    20 points

    Did you explain what the product does? (Not what it would do or will do.) Does the abstract answer the three questions it was supposed to answer (without addressing the questions)?

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

  • Audience

    20 points

    Did you make it clear who you are designing this product for? Did you include characteristics that are relevant to the product use rather than demographic data?

    • 20 pts Satisfactory
    • 15 pts Mostly satisfactory
    • 10 pts Partially satisfactory
    • 0 pts Unsatisfactory

Submission

  1. Submit your PDF to Canvas using the name convention groupname-m1.pdf.

  2. Always submit the whole document as it is now. One submission per group is sufficient.

  3. Good typography will be always required and expected in this class.

  4. Please check Canvas for deadline.
