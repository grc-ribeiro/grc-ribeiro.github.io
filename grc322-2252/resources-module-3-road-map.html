GRC 322: Resources

Module 3

Working with grids

Type hierarchy (cont.)

Learning objectives

In this module we will work on how to:

  1. Use typographical grid to organize content.

  2. Select the appropriate type families for a layout.

  3. Apply proper visual hierarchy to match content hierarchy.

Before the lab

  1. Watch the video on type hierarchy and complete its quiz on Canvas.
  2. Watch the video on working with grids and complete its quiz on Canvas.
  3. Watch the video with examples of the assignment.
  4. Research the designer assigned to you. Write one introductory text (150–200) about them, find a good portrait of them, and collect 12 to 30 high-quality images of their work.
  5. Build an InDesign file with grid options on blank pages.

Entry ticket

Before you come to class you need to complete the following:

  1. Finish the quiz on the topic of working with grid.
  2. Submit a short introductory text (150–200 words) about your designer
  3. Submit a zip file with 12 to 30 high-quality images for the assignment.
  4. Submit an InDesign file with five 12″ × 18″ (vertical) pages featuring grids with 6, 7, 8, 9, and 10 columns.

All due the night before lab at 11:59pm.

Come prepared and energized to work on your poster!

In the lab

  1. The remaining of the class will be dedicated to working on your poster. Expect collaboration with your peers and mini-critiques from me.

  2. In the last 30 minutes of class, we’ll review everybody’s work, so you can make the final changes at home.

After the lab

Submit the assignment. (Deadline assigned on Canvas.)

And don’t forget to submit your journals!
