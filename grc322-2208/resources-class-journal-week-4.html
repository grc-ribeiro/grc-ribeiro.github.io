GRC 322: Resources

Class journal, week 4

I enjoy the organization and structure of this class, having the calendar allows me to stay on top of my assignments. I also appreciate how transparent it is.

I don’t like surprises and I assume you wouldn’t like them either.

This weeks project is so fun to learn about this different artists and what work I have seen of theirs in the past without knowing who the artist was!

They are all amazing designers. But they’re not artists; they’re designers.

“The grid is the underwear of the book.” -Massimo Vignelli.

I don’t really love this quote. I think the analogy is silly, a little distasteful, and it doesn’t make much sense.

I am glad this week we are working through InDesign, I struggle a bit more when having to code.

I’d argue that last assignment was mostly done in a browser and text editor (for the research) and in Illustrator for comparison. Throwing everything on a web page should be trivial at this point.

I really enjoyed this weeks assignment. I didn’t want to stop working on it.

I’m so happy to read this. I too didn’t want to stop working on my examples.

I really appreciate how you demo the techniques in InDesign and clearly show which options to select and where we can find them in. It saves a lot of time that I would have spent Googling for all the keyboard shortcuts.

Don’t stop googling, though!

Massimo Vignelli draws all his designs out first before getting near a computer.

He did. And I know several designers and educators that do the same and require the same. It never worked for me. It doesn’t help me; I’m faster on the computer, so I don’t do use pencil and paper, except for logos. But I’m glad others teachers require students to sketch on paper first, so you get to try different methods and later you will use what works best for you.

Sometimes, completely restarting on a design is valuable, especially when you realize that the design likely won’t work.

It’s absolutely part of the process. I wish famous designers would share how bad their first idea can be. But I guess even they are embarrassed to show their first iterations.

I found it hard to be original in my grid design, since with the measurements, a round number of 3 made the most sense for a 12” W. It made my poster’s margins too small or too large.

You can use fractions of inches down to 0.125. I use the metrical system, which is vastly superior, with multiples of 3mm, 4mm, or 5mm, depending on the project.

What is a good general rule of thumb for leading size? I can’t quite remember what was recommended in 318.

It’s usually somewhere between 120% and 140%. Bigger the type, smaller this percentage needs to be, though.
