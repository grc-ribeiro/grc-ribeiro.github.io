GRC 322: Assignments

Type comparison (posters, second round)

The design process is not linear, it consists of constantly expanding options and selecting the best. The first round was the first exploration, where each one of you expanded ideas. Expanding works best individually, without the constraints from teammates.

But selecting works best in groups, because that’s when you analyze and discuss solutions. For this second round, then, you will go back to your original group (from the type comparison webpage) to share your ideas for the poster. Compare solutions and feedback, share files, and discuss strategies. Forget ownership; it doesn’t matter who did what poster, you want to collectively find the two or three best candidates.

After selecting, it’s time to expanding ideas again, so you will go back to working individually, developing your own design or one of your teammates’.

(For the final round next week, there’ll be only one poster for each team, but now, you want two or three posters for each person.)

Tasks

  1. Read the feedback from the first round.

  2. Meet with your group, but leave your ego outside.

  3. Share your files and feedback with your group. In this stage, it’s important not to be defensive of your ideas. You’re working collectively to find the best solution.

  4. During the meeting, don’t open InDesign. Discuss the solutions verbally or through rough sketches.

  5. Individually, work on the two ideas the group decided that was the best to go with.

  6. If you want to, work on a third idea that the group didn’t see potential but you do.

Requirements

Same as round one.

  1. Format

    24″ wide
    36″ tall
    Horizontal posters are an automatic zero.

  2. History of each typeface

    For each typeface, include facts such as who designed it, when, and where. Also, explain if there was a specific purpose for its design and the historical context of its creation.

1. Comparison
---------------------
Explain, through images and diagrams, what are the biggest similarities and big differences between the two typefaces assigned to your group.

1. Style
----------------
For the main text use a typeface that is very different from both typefaces you are researching. Suggestion: if your typefaces are sans-serif, use a serif typeface for your copy; and vice-versa.

(Don’t include the visual references.)

Style tips

Same as round one.

  1. Just as on the webpage, make sure your lines don’t get longer than 60 characters.

  2. Turn-off hyphenation.

  3. Check for widows.

  4. You don’t want indentation on your poster. It’s always either indentation or skipping a line. On a book, you use indentation; on a poster, you skip a line. But never both.

  5. Check your hyphens and dashes.

  6. Don’t use default fonts. You probably have access to Adobe Typekit, use it.

Evaluation

Check the rubric on Canvas. Read the long description as well as each criterion.

If you can’t see it, please let me know as soon as possible.

Submission

  1. Export each of your layouts twice, once showing the grid, and once without it. (If you have different baseline grids, you may have to work in different InDesign files and combine the PDFs.)

  2. Make a 2‑or‑3‑page PDF document with all the pages exported, with the grids hidden. Name it using the name convention lastname-type-poster-2-grid-hidden.pdf.

  3. Make a 2‑or‑3‑page PDF document with all the pages exported, with the grids showing. Name it using the name convention lastname-type-poster-2-grid-show.pdf.

  4. Submit both files to Canvas by Monday, 9 November 2020, at 11:59pm Wednesday, 11 November 2020, at 11:59pm.

This second submission will count towards your final grade for the course.
