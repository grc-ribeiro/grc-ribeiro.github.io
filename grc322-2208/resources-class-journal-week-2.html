Will we be using and learning more about InDesign like we did in GrC 318? I found that aspect of that class to be useful and was wondering how 322 will compare to 318 since they are both digital typography.

It’s not as InDesign heavy, but I’ll try to give some InDesign tips every now and then.

Meeting with you on Zoom REALLY helped, I will definitely be meeting with you more often if I am confused!

Please do.

Thank you for having such flexible office hours, scheduling on Calendly was so easy and I really appreciated getting some feedback on the pixel font assignment in person.

It’s my first time using Calendly. I didn’t want to use something new. But since everybody else is using, I figured it was the easiest for you students.

Also thank you for making your videos concise, to the point, and digestible. I truly look forward to watching them knowing I will be receiving valuable information.

Thanks for the kind words. Let me know if you feel they are not as valuable anymore, though.

I think other people have mentioned this, but having shorter, concise videos for lectures is extremely helpful. Long videos that are half an hour to an hour long are difficult to get through while staying attentive or even staying awake. And when there is a piece of information you missed or want to go back to at a later date, you have to search and scroll through the entire video just to get what you need. Shorter videos keep things less overwhelming, easier to remember, and easier to go back and find anything that needs further explanation.

I’m glad it’s working for most people.

As frustrating as it is to struggle with what seemed like a simple assignment, nothing great was made easily right?

Struggle is normal, but I didn’t mean it to be as frustrating as it seems to be the case.

I loved how we were given an additional submission for the pixel assignment. I appreciate how you were able to pivot your schedule to help us best learn and review the assignment. Creating the additional video was very helpful!

My first time teaching this class online. And my first time giving this assignment in years. We’re all making adjustments. Thanks for your patience.

I think the reason I was so off in doing the pixel assignment was I was focusing more on designing something unique rather than designing for readability/legibility. Now I know it’s more about readability/legibility and a little personality.

You got it!

I’m still not fully visualizing the disconnected letters from the video as more legible; to me it makes it harder to read.

I don’t think they’re more legible. They’re legible enough and a valid compromise if you want to keep the descenders. But to design is to make decision.

In the future, I think you should include a version of the numbers in the gray color scheme in the assignment description. I think that would have cleared up some confusion.

I will! Thanks for the suggestion!

It was painful to see my grade from my first attempt at the pixel font assignment. I knew that some of my letters were not perfect, but I was not expecting that mark. Thank you for giving us another chance to turn in a draft.

I didn’t mean it to be painful, but it was never meant to count towards final grade. Like you said, the only thing I changed was the second opportunity for a draft before the final submission.

My next step for improving my font was printing out the grids on paper. I have always worked better by hand with pencil and paper and trying to brainstorm and create a font in illustrator did not work for me. Sketching things by hand is an important first step for me when designing or creating anything. I can then take my sketches and transfer that to illustrator.

That’d have been my first step too to be honest. I may include this suggestions in the future.

The submissions I saw also had interesting methods of getting around the low pixel count maximum.

That’s what I call creativity. Creativity is way more interesting when you have limitations.

With no videos or quizzes this week, it was good to focus just on the pixel font assignment. As many of us have found out, it sounds like there is going to be a lot of doing and redoing in this class.

That’s the design process.

This week I learned that I need some peer support for feedback on my work.

Please do collaborate! But don’t forget I can help you during office hours. You can even come in groups to the office hours, and that has proved to be helpful in the past.

Are there any references you recommend if we want to learn more about typefaces/typeface design?

I am not an expert in typeface design. We’ll discuss typefaces in the next assignment. But if you that’s a career you want to pursue—and I don’t recommend it—, I may have some connections that could mentor you.

I did not realize how difficult it is to keep all the letters cohesive and similar.

Now imagine actually design a full typeface, with lowercase, uppercase, numbers, punctuation, all the glyphs. Then a full type family, with italics and weights…

For every assignment, is the first submission not counting to our actual grade? Or, was it just for this one?

Not all of them. I’ll let you know when the first draft is just a draft.
