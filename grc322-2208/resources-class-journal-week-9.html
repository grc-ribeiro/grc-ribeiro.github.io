Class journal, week 9

I wanted to say again that I really like the format of this class; I appreciate that the information is concise and there isn’t unnecessary busy work.

Never waxing‑on and waxing‑off with me.

I plan to schedule a meeting with you this week before the final submission, in order to clarify a few questions I still have with my solutions and their overall effectiveness. I think that it would also be a good idea to have [my team] join as well, comparing the strengths/weaknesses and deciding which layout to base our final poster off of.

Sounds like a good idea.

The office hours were very helpful. I feel like I learned a lot from the office hours.

I know you did!

Your point of making the posters something that we want to put up on our walls has helped me be more creative, instead of just trying to cram all the information on the page.

But remember… “Don’t try to be original, just try to be good.”

I did not know that someone else was going to have my files. I wish I was more organized creating my files for the first submission. Now I know to clean things up before it’s “finished”.

Always keep your files organized.

I’ve been able to put my InDesign skills to use for my job and it’s helped me to reorganize and redesign several outdated and unorganized documents for my team.

You’re doing God’s work.

I enjoyed going back to our original group to get some fresh ideas.

Keep that in mind next time you’r collaborating. It works much better if everybody brings semi‑developed ideas to the table.

It was interesting working on someone else’s design files, but also gave me a look into how I could set up my documents different.

Each person will work on files differently, but it is a good idea to take ideas from other people’s workflow.

This week, I worked with my group and independently on the type comparison posters. I felt it was a lot easier to make adjustments to my group mate’s work, because I wasn’t as married to the original design. I had a lot of trouble reimagining my original poster, which was the other poster my group worked on because I felt it had a strong layout.

It is usually easier to improve other people’s ideas than your own’s.

I’ve heard about the type comparison midterm. It’s famous! :D

Last year, a student embarrassed me when when she took the midterm for fun in the resource room and got 100% without being enrolled in the class.

Indentation works better with justified alignment, whereas skipping a line works better with right-ragged text.

You got it.

I am worried how much work I will have for this class during Thanksgiving break.

Enough to give you an excuse not to participate in those conversations about the pandemic or the election. You’re welcome.
