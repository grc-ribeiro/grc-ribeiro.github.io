GRC 322: Assignments

Type comparison (final posters)

As promissed in the second round, now you go back to your group to work on one final poster for the group.

Tasks

  1. Read the feedback from the first and second round.

  2. Watch this short video on important typographic details.

  3. Meet with your group, but leave your ego outside.

  4. Share your files and feedback with your group. Once again, it’s important not to be defensive of your ideas. You’re working collectively to find the best solution.

  5. During the meeting, don’t open InDesign. Discuss the solutions verbally or through rough sketches.

  6. Individually, work on the idea the group decided that was the best to go with.

  7. Go back to the group to decide on the best final execution and polish the details. (You probably want to share your screen and work on InDesign now.)

Requirements

Same as round one and two.

  1. Format

    24″ wide
    36″ tall
    Horizontal posters are an automatic zero.

  2. History of each typeface

    For each typeface, include facts such as who designed it, when, and where. Also, explain if there was a specific purpose for its design and the historical context of its creation.

  3. Comparison

    Explain, through images and diagrams, what are the biggest similarities and big differences between the two typefaces assigned to your group.

  4. Style

    For the main text use a typeface that is very different from both typefaces you are researching. Suggestion: if your typefaces are sans-serif, use a serif typeface for your copy; and vice-versa.

(Don’t include the visual references.)

Style tips

Same as round one and two.

  1. Just as on the webpage, make sure your lines don’t get longer than 60 characters.

  2. Turn-off hyphenation.

  3. Check for widows.

  4. Check your rags carefully.

  5. You don’t want indentation on your poster. It’s always either indentation or skipping a line. On a book, you use indentation; on a poster, you skip a line. But never both.

  6. Check your hyphens and dashes.

  7. Don’t use default fonts. You probably have access to Adobe Typekit, use it.

Evaluation

Check the rubric on Canvas. Read the long description as well as each criterion.

If you can’t see it, please let me know as soon as possible.

Submission

  1. Export your poster twice, once showing the grid, and once without it.

  2. Name the file without the grid using the name convention lastname-lastname-lastname-type-poster-3-grid-hidden.pdf.

  3. Name the file with the grid using the name convention lastname-lastname-lastname-type-poster-3-grid-show.pdf.

  4. Submit both files to Canvas by Wednesday, 18 November 2020, at 11:59pm.

(No design decision explanation necessary.)
