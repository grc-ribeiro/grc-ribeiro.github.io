GRC 322: Assignments

Designer poster

Based on the list below, design a poster for the designer assigned to you. Make sure the layout emphasize the grid.

Tasks

  1. Research the life and work of the designer assigned to you.

  2. Collect images in high resolution.

  3. Design six versions of a 12″ × 18″ vertical (portrait) poster:
    You want to use* three different grids* with either 6, 7, 8, 9, or 10 columns.
    For each option of grid, design two layouts, with different placement of elements and different typefaces.

  4. Speaking of typefaces, make sure you research the type families you’re planning on using. You want to use well regarded type families. Once again, the excellent Fonts In Use website can be a very helpful starting point.

Requirements

  1. 12″ × 18″ vertical (portrait)

    By the way, if we’re talking about design work, first number is always the width, and second is height. But for art work, it’s the opposite. I didn’t make these rules.

  2. White background and no bleeding images

    I plan to print them eventually and bleeding colors would make it much harder.

  3. Name of the designer as the title of the poster

    Typeset in 32pt or bigger

  4. Short introductory text 150–200 words

  5. Portrait of the designer in grayscale

  6. Example of their work in colors

    Except for when the original isn’t in colors, of course.

  7. Captions for all work examples

    Make sure you have proper hierarchy for those.

Optional

  1. Official URL if available

    Wikipedia article or other third-party pages are not an official website.

  2. Designer’s Twitter and/or Instagram handle if available

    Again, only if it’s official and maintained by the designer themselves or someone on their team.

  3. Quotes from the designer

    Some designers have some interesting quotes, and they can be look nice on the poster.

Submission

  1. Export each of your layout twice, once showing the grid, and once without it.

  2. Make a 6‑page PDF document with all the pages exported, with the grids hidden. Name it using the name convention lastname-grid-hidden.pdf.

  3. Make a 6‑page PDF document with all the pages exported, with the grids showing. Name it using the name convention lastname-grid-show.pdf.

  4. Submit both files to Canvas by Monday, 12 October 2020, at 11:59pm.
