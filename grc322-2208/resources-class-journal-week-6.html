Class journal, week 6

Your assignment breakdowns are also very clear and concise.  If I’m confused about something, I can easily scroll through the different sections of the assignment and find what I’m looking for, and the entire assignment sheet has a clear hierarchy.

I’m always trying to be as clear as possible. Sometimes, students will come back with questions, and I realize I could’ve been more clear. Then I learn from my mistakes too.

The “International Typographic Style” poster in the Research Poster video was very interesting to me and definitely caught my eye.

I love that poster. I need a copy of it.

Designing for such a large size is much different than what we have previously been working on. It is nice to have so much space but I have to remind myself just because it’s there I don’t need to fill every inch.

All true. It is very different. Also, you don’t need to fill every inch.

I think learning to create an interesting academic/ research poster is a useful skill for the future as it can be applied to many different projects and presentations. It makes conveying information convenient and interesting.

It really is.

It was a relief to get a good grade on the Designer Poster final submission. I spent a really long time going over all the comments/rubric and tried to be as detailed as I could in the explanation. I’m glad that the effort was reflected in my grade.

More important, the effort was reflected in your work.

I am excited for the font comparison poster, and I hope that I like mine enough to print it out and put it on my own wall.

I too hope I like yours to print it out and put it on my wall. I used to have some posters from this assignment on my wall. I should print some new ones.

I think it’s really cool how we are now combining what we learned from the last two projects into another project. Really reinforces what we are learning in this class.

And the results are showing it!

I liked having to write out the explanations for our design decisions last week. Some of the explanations I’d thought out beforehand as I was designing, but I had to sit down and think about a lot of them, and it helped me mess with the final poster until I liked it.

That was the idea. To write is to think.

Like last week, I appreciate that you show examples of past students’ work. It’s incredibly helpful to reference someone else’s work when you’ve never designed anything like a research poster before.

Try not to look only on my examples, though. Always look for references from good designers when working. The list for the designers’ posters assignment is a good start.

An entry point is a point of physical or attentional entry into a design. This is what usually catches your attention first upon first-glance.

In looking at research poster examples on my own, I noticed many of them fail to include an effective point of entry.

Yes. That’s a big part of it. And lack of blank space.

For the grid on our poster, I was confused about how much we can expand our stylistic elements. If I am bleeding a title or large element, how do I still align to my grid?

When you bleed, you break the grid. And it’s fine.

I just wanted you to know that I was working on a powerpoint for senior project this week and couldn’t bring myself to center-align any of my text. So. You’ve had in impact. :p

And it’s hard, isn’t it? It’s tempting to just center everything and feel like you’ve done something with type. Not centering is harder, but worth it. Nothing great comes easy, etc.

I still get confused on using grids for different sized fonts. If I use a font size that doesn’t match with the horizontal grid, should I still use it? Or do I always need to edit the leading/font point size so that the text lines up somewhere with the horizontal grid?

I edit everything to make the design more systematic.

I really enjoy seeing other peoples work, will we be able to see everyones completed posters at the end?

They’ll be your study material for the midterm.
