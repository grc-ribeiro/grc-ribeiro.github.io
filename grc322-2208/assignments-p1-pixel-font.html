GRC 322: Assignments

Pixel font

For this assignment, you will design ten characters of a pixel font. In my example below, I took a five-pixel tall grid and design a set of numerals.

The block numbers in the example above are functional, but lazy. The letterforms in the example below are more organic and they would probably harmonize better with upper and lowercase letters of a typeface.

In this case, the second set compromises a little on legibility to increase the readability. In other words, the numbers on the second set are not as easily recognizable individually, but are more enjoyable as a set.

Tasks

Design 10 characters of a typeface (H, a, n, d, g, l, o, v, e, s) and represent them in four different ways:

  1. 6-square height; black and white only.

  2. 9-square height; black and white only.

  3. 6-square height; black, white, and two shades of gray of your choice.

  4. 9-square height; black, white, and two shades of gray of your choice.

Your goal is to design those letters for legibility, consistency, and readability.

Evaluation

  • Legibility

    Each character is easily recognizable for what it’s supposed to be.
    10 points for each set.

  • Readability

    The set is enjoyable to read.
    10 points for each set.

  • Consistency

    The letterform of each character is consistent with the set.
    5 points for each set.

Submission

Download this template and place your font on the guides.

Submit it to Canvas as a PDF file, using the name convention lastname-pixel-font.pdf. (Replace lastname with your actual last name, please.)
