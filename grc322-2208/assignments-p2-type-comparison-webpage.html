GRC 322: Assignments

Type comparison (webpage)

Research

For this assignment, each group will be assigned with one pair of typefaces from this list:

Bodoni vs Didot

Franklin Gothic vs Trade Gothic

Adobe Garamond vs Minion

Helvetica Neue vs Aktiv Grotesk

Sabon vs Times New Roman

Clarendon vs Sutro

DIN vs Interstate

Rockwell vs Serifa

Meta vs Officina

Baskerville vs Caslon

Futura vs Mr Eaves Sans

Tasks

  1. Extensively research and compare the two typefaces.

  2. Build a webpage built with HTML and one CSS external file explaining your findings to your peers.

Requirements

  1. History of each typeface

    For each typeface, include facts such as who design it, when, and where. Also, explain if there was a specific purpose for its design and the historical context of its creation.

  2. Comparison

    Explain, through images and diagrams, what are the biggest similarities and big differences between the two typefaces assigned to your group.

  3. Examples and visual references

    Research images where the use of the typefaces were particularly successful. Fonts In Use can be a very helpful starting point.

  4. Style

    For the main text use a typeface that is very different from both typefaces you are researching. Suggestion: if your typefaces are sans-serif, use a serif typeface for your copy; and vice-versa.

Evaluation

Content

  • History

    Are the facts context for each typeface properly described and explained on the page? Did you include type designer, year, and location? Did you try to set political and artistic context in which the typeface was designed and released?
    20 points

  • Comparison

    Did you do a thorough comparison of the typefaces, including uppercase, lowercase, punctuation, and numerals? Is the comparison effective? Would someone be able to distinguish them after reading your webpage?
    30 points

  • Examples and visual references

    Did you include a good variety of the use of the typefaces? Are they legit source of good design? Did you research logos, advertising, and publications?
    20 points

Presentation

  • Alignment; Hierarchy

    Does the placement of elements create a sense of unity and cohesion, contributing to the design’s overall aesthetic and perceived stability? Does the alignment of elements correctly lead the user through the page? Does the visual hierarchy represent the content hierarchy?
    5 points

  • Color; Highlighting; Consistency; Similarity

    Is color being used effectively to direct users to attract attention, group elements, indicate meaning, and enhance aesthetics? Are similar parts related? Are related parts similar? Are the variations consistently applied in all parts of the website?
    5 points

  • HTML and CSS

    Did you apply proper HTML tags according to the semantic hierarchy of the document? Did you use proper, semantic CSS properties? Did you avoid the mistakes from the GRC 338 infamous list?
    20 points

Style tips

  1. On your CSS, start with this line:
    h1, h2, h3, h4, p, ul, ol, li {margin: 0; padding:0; font-size: 1em; font-weight: normal;}
    (Watch the video to understand why.)

  2. Don’t use pure white background and pure black type. If you want your page to look like it’s black on white, use a very dark on a very light gray. (I use #333333 and #fafafa on this very page.)

  3. Don’t use default fonts. You probably have access to Adobe Typekit, use it.

  4. Give your page a max-width so your lines don’t get longer than 60 characters.
