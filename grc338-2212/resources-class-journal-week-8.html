I added an HTML plugin to Squarespace and they used rem as a unit of measurement. Is that just the same as em?

Great question! rem and em are not exactly the same, but close. “rem” stands for “root em”. So that means that 1rem is the font size of the html element. In most browsers, it’s 16px, but the reason we use em instead of px for everything is to adapt to different preferences, in case someone chooses their browser to show fonts bigger or smaller. By default, paragraphs are 1em and I ask you to keep them to respect the user’s preference. But let’s say you styled your h2 as 2.5em, which is reasonable. Then, if you apply margin-left: 1.25em to both p and h2, you would expect them to be aligned to the left, but shifted to the right. Well, that won’t happen. p will move to 1.25em to the right, but h2 will move 3.125em to the right, because its em is worth 2.5× the original em. So instead, you want to apply margin-left: 1.25rem to both p and h2 so they are always aligned. In the end, rem is a unit that’s easier to use than em, and you’re welcome to use it if it makes sense to you.
