GRC 338: Assignments

Design principles examples

Every week, we’ll be discussing four different design principles from the Universal principles of design book. The book can be read online for free through the Cal Poly library.

After taking the quiz about the four principles of the week, check the group discussion on Canvas. Each week, you will be randomly assigned to a different group, and each group is assigned to a different principle. (Twelve groups total, three groups per topic, but you only need to work with your own group.) Each student is expected to work on the quiz and then discuss examples with the group. If I don’t see any participation in the group discussion board, I will have to assume that you didn’t work on the assignment.

If you don’t participate by Tuesday night, I’ll drop you from the group on Wednesday morning, and you’ll receive a zero for the week’s discussion. (At the end of the quarter, though, your lowest two grades for the weekly discussions will be discarded.)

Post on the discussion

To participate in the discussion, you must find at least two images to represent the principle assigned to your group. One image should be of a physical product and the other should show a website. (An app is not a website.)

For the physical product, magazine pages, billboards, and even logos are acceptable. But also, of course, a real object like a car or a keychain.

No example designed by Apple, Google, or Netflix is allowed in either category.

Submit a very brief explanation on why you think they’re good examples. At this point, a few bullet points are sufficient.

Assignment submission

As a group, discuss what are the most effective examples for the physical and web examples.

Write one paragraph for each image, explaining why it is an example of good design and good use of the principle. Use the theory of the book as well as your own words. Submit your paragraph descriptions as online text on Canvas. Do not write your name, the name of the principle, date, or anything. Write two and only two paragraphs per submission: in the first paragraph, you write about the physical example; in the second paragraph, you write about the website example.

Submit your text as comments for the assignment on Canvas.

Submit your text as a .txt file on Canvas. Use the name convention of the word group, followed by your group number.

Each image should be 1200px wide, at least.

Read these instructions on how to take a good screenshot on a Mac. Somebody in the group should have access to a Mac. (This is by far the main reason why students miss points in this assignment.)

Submit two images to Canvas using this name convention:

  1. The word group, followed by your group number.
  2. A hyphen(-) (not underscore).
  3. Number 1 for the physical example; or 2 for the web example.
  4. An optional sequential lowercase letter, in case you have additional images for the same example.

(If you want to submit more than one image, just add lowercase letters after the numbers: group1-1a.png, group1-1b.png, etc., but keep the physical examples as #1 and web design examples as #2.)

Note: If you have to resubmit a file, Canvas won’t let you replace it keeping the original name. But that’s the system’s fault, not yours. If you catch a mistake after uploading the file, go ahead and resubmit it. Don’t worry about the extra number in the end. If the name convention is correct otherwise, I’ll understand.

Example

I wrote two examples of submissions, so you can have a better idea of how to write your paragraphs and how to name your image files.

Weekly timeline

On Mondays, the quiz, the discussion, and the assignment will be open on Canvas. I’d suggest taking the quizzes on Mondays.

On Tuesdays, you should be engaging in the asynchronous, online discussion on Canvas.

On Wednesdays, someone in your group will be submitting the assignment for the whole group on Canvas.

On Thursdays, I will grade all the submissions.

On Fridays, I will post a video with the analysis of each example and some examples of my own.
