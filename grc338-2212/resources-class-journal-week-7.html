Class journal, week 7

And as a last note, I’ve been enjoying reading through everyone’s journal entry highlights! It’s nice to see what my peers are working on or thoughts on this class.

I always enjoy reading them, but even more now that’s online.

The Cal Poly User Experience club is hosting Professor Erin Sheets this Tuesday, February 23rd at 11 AM PST! Prof. Sheets will be talking about how we can design for authenticity, connection, and truth in a world that is become more digital than ever. Free to register and attend!.

Thanks for sharing, Barrett!

I like change and it has been tough doing the same assignments every week. At first, I appreciated the consistency, but almost every week has been the same for all my classes, which can get old.

If we were in person-to-person, I could change it up a little, but in this online environment, I’d rather have you bored than confused, though.

A little bit confused about containers and formatting grids.

Office hours sound like your best option to get help, then.

The power of the grid had eluded me until this last Monday where I finally asked for help. Thank you!

My job is not to teach you, but to help you all learn.

I need to make sure to check the list of mistakes to avoid before turning in my assignments.

Please do! I really don’t like to apply penalties to otherwise good work.

It was nice to be able to take a break from some of my other work to focus on coding. I found it surprisingly stress‑reliving.

It can be! You kind of zone out.

FreeFormatter.com has been helping me format my HTML in some of the longer projects like last week’s assignment.

It’s a great help for you to check your mistakes and for me to read your code.

When writing in the <p> I found it very helpful to first write it in a google doc then copy-paste so you can have spell check.

Not a bad idea. You can also copy the page content and place it in Grammarly.

I want to learn how to make a nav bar with a surface that follows the scroll of the mouse.

It’s not hard. But I advise against it.

I intend to take inspo from pinterest and other coffeeshop websites to incorporate some elements similar.

Sounds like a smart idea!

For performance load I don’t quite understand kinematic load. is there a better example than doing morse code with telegraphs.

The barcode example on the video is a good example.

I learned that Fitt’s Law is more about how the placement elements can make it easier for a user to get to them quickly, not about how easily they are noticed.

Correct.

I liked the barcode example for Performance Load. I never considered how much work would have to be done if it wasn’t easy to just scan a product.

And I didn’t mention that once in Brazil, we had inflation as high as 80% in one month. So somebody was constantly remarking the food. A nightmare for everybody involved.

One of the things I’ve noticed is that all of these design principles we’ve been discussing are, at their core, psychology principles. It’s been interesting to be able to apply the knowledge from the cognitive psychology class I’m in to this class!

Design is always about people. So is psychology.

Regardless, I am excited to use my knowledge in this class and the design principles I’ve learned into presenting my case studies. I always found it difficult to explain my design choices but knowing terms and principles have definitely made it easier.

It really helps, right? Even if you designed using the principles without knowing their names, now it’s easier to explain your decisions.

HTML is getting more complex and I need to be more detail-oriented when it comes to instructions and hierarchy. I lost points and it made me sad, but now I am motivated to improve on the next project.

That’s the right attitude! And don’t be sad about the points. You’ve all noticed that each assignment is worth very little and it is the sum of them all that will make you succeed in this class.

Is it better to import fonts to our HTML document by downloading the font files first and linking them or by linking a URL?

You want to use Google Fonts or, even better, Adobe Fonts.

This week I learned that every alt attribute should end with a period!

Yes. It helps the life of a blind person at least a little.

I thought that the midterm was so reasonable and a great way to help recall all of the coding terminology and rules!

“Reasonable” is a nice way to describe it.

The midterm was a super helpful review! I feel really confident in my understanding of HTML and CSS terminology now.

That was the goal!

I am nervous to style the website because that is the most difficult part for me.

Don’t be nervous about style. Follow the rubric and the principles.

Looking ahead, I am interested to see what the responsive website HTML/CSS assignment entails.

We’ll make the same page adaptive to any screen size. Stay tuned.

Everything seems to be ramping up now that the quarter is almost over. I’m almost sad to think we’re almost done!

I may teach GRC 339 in the summer, though. Stay tuned.
