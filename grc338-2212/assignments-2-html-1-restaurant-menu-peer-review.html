GRC 338: Assignments

Restaurant menu peer review

On Friday, January 15th, you will be assigned to peer review two webpages from other students.

Use the list of mistakes to avoid as a checklist.

This is an opportunity for you to help your colleagues since they will have the chance to fix the mistakes you catch before I grade their assignments.

Your task

  1. Open the list of mistakes to avoid on one browser window.

  2. Navigate to the page with all the students’ links on another window.

  3. Click on the names of the students assigned to you.

  4. Open the source code. (See below)

  5. Write comments for each mistake you find, using the following format:
    Line 8: You forgot to close the h1 tag.
    Line 14: The heading of the list should not be part of the list. Only list items can be part of the list.

  6. Check for additional mistakes of hierarchy:
    Line 10: This should be an h2, not h1.

  7. Feel free to add suggestions or compliments.

  8. Repeat for the second student.

How to see the source code of a webpage

macOS

Firefox: ⌘U (Command+U)
Chrome: ⌥⌘U (Option+Command+U)
Safari: ⌥⌘U (Option+Command+U)

Windows

Firefox: CTRL+U
Internet Explorer: CTRL+U
Edge: CTRL+U
Chrome: CTRL+U
Opera: CTRL+U

Evaluation

Peer reviews count for 10% of your final grade in this class.

Each peer review assigned to you is worth 100 points.

100 points: Peer review is complete. Enough mistakes were caught or there were complements to the student.
50 points: Peer review was attempted, but easy mistakes were not caught.
0 points: No attempt to peer review.
