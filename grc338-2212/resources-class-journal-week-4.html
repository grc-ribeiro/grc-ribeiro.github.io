Even though my group was assigned the form follows function design principle this week, I was having a really hard time understanding it. Because the textbook stated that the descriptive interpretation was false, I was under the impression that this principle is something that we should not follow. After seeing my group’s examples and the analysis video though, I see that there are examples of this principle that work well. I was wondering if I could have some clarification on form follows function?

The descriptive interpretation is not false. What is false is the belief that, in nature, form follows function. But as the book explains, “functional criteria represent a more objective aesthetic than alternative approaches. The result is designs that are more timeless and enduring, but also frequently perceived by general audiences as simple and uninteresting.” I dispute the authors’ statement about the frequent perception of the general audience, especially for the vagueness of the words “frequently” and “general”. But the main point is to design with the function as the main target.
