GRC 322: Assignments

Kinetic typography

This is a group assignment will be consisted of making a 90-to-120-second, informational, and educational movie.

The theme is open, but must be relevant to your audience. The broader the audience, the better.

The narrative must be based on facts, not opinions; and quantitative data, rather than qualitative data.

Constraints

You may use type, motion, shapes, colors, sounds, music, voice, live video, or any element to tell the story. Emphasis on type in motion.

You may use copyright-free, live action footage or vector, 2D illustration. This is not an animation course. If you’re using illustration, don’t try to make it realistic. Make it graphical, instead.

Music is required and sound effects are strongly advised.

(You are encouraged to have fun with the assignment, but it doesn’t mean you want it to be funny. Avoid jokes, puns, etc.)

Evaluation

You will be evaluated on the quality of your story, visual language, motion effects, and sound.

 

References page

As part of the process, each group needs to present their references for the project. I recommend using a board on Pinterest with 30 to 70 images and videos. (Make sure you have an exclusive board for the project.)

The refererence page counts for 10% of the grade on this assignment.

PolyLearn: Submit a link to your references until Friday, 10 November 2018, at 4:00 pm.

Stills

Using any software of your choice, design high-definition, still images of two or three frames of your video, to show the visual language. (Two or three screens per student.)

Stills count for 10% of the grade on this assignment.

Individual submissions to PolyLearn: Submit a multi-page PDF until Tuesday, 13 November 2018, at 8:00am, using the name convention lastname-stills.pdf.

Script

As part of the process, each group should write a detailed script of their movie. Include all the text (on screen and narration), description of the images, and estimated timeframe. (You can find examples from previous years on PolyLearn.)

The script counts for 10% of the grade on this assignment.

Individual submissions to PolyLearn: Submit a multi-page PDF until Tuesday, 13 November 2018, at 8:00am, using the name convention lastname-script.pdf.

First draft

As part of the process, a first draft of your project is due on November 18th. This draft should consist of a series of videos (one movie per student), with iconography, colors, and typography as intended as final. It’s fine, however, if it lacks any motion. You may think of it as a playable slideshow, or roughly one frame every two or three seconds, as needed. Also, all choices made to this point are open to discussion before final submission.

The first draft counts for 15% of your grade on this assignment.

Individual submissions to PolyLearn: Submit a link to your video on Vimeo or YouTube until Saturday, 18 November 2018, at 4:00 pm.

Second draft

As part of the process, a second draft of your project is due on November 29th. This draft should consist of a full-length video, with iconography, colors, typography, and motion as intended as final. It’s fine, however, if it lacks refinement and secondary animation. Also, all choices made to this point are open to discussion, of course.

The second draft counts for 25% of your grade on this assignment.

Group submissions to PolyLearn: Submit a link to your video on Vimeo or YouTube until Friday, 30 November 2018, at 11:55 pm.

Final deliverable

The final deliverable is a 90-to-120-second movie, with 1920×1080, 30 fps, produced in Adobe After Effects.

The final deliverable counts for 30% of your grade for this assignment.

Group submissions to PolyLearn: Submit a link to your video on Vimeo or YouTube until Thursday, 6 December 2018, at 8:00 am.
