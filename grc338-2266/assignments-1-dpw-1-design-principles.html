GRC 338: Assignments

Design principles examples

Every week, we’ll be discussing four different design principles from the Universal principles of design book. The book can be read online for free through the Cal Poly library.

Tasks

  1. Check the on Canvas what principle is assigned to you this week.

  2. Find one physical example to represent the principle assigned to you. For the physical product, magazine pages, billboards, and even logos are acceptable. But also, of course, a real object like a car or a pencil.

  3. Find one website example to represent the principle assigned to you. (An app is not a website.)

  4. Each image should be 1200px wide, at least.

  5. Submit both images with an explanation on why you think they’re good examples. (Don’t email me them for any reason.) If you believe that you need more than one image to fully show your examples, you’re encouraged to submit as many images as you need.

  6. Write one paragraph for each image, explaining why it is an example of good design and good use of the principle. Use the theory of the book as well as your own words.

  7. Submit your paragraph descriptions as a text file on Canvas. Write two and only two paragraphs per submission: in the first paragraph, you write about the physical example; in the second paragraph, you write about the website example. Submit your text as a .txt file on Canvas.

  8. For the web example, read these instructions on how to take a good screenshot on a Mac. (This is by far the main reason why students miss points in this assignment.)

  9. For the physical example, you don’t want a browser screenshot; just submit a nice image.

No example designed by Apple, Google, or Netflix is allowed in either category.

Example

I wrote two examples of submissions, so you can have a better idea of how to write your paragraphs and how to name your image files.

Evaluation

I’ll grade you submission on outcome not effort.
