GRC 338: Resources

Class journal, week 5

I can’t believe this class is almost over! I feel like we’ve learned so much since the “Hello World” assignment. I am surprised that we were able to advance from having little to no HTML knowledge to creating our own styled and interactive websites!

I hope you have enjoyed it!

There was a restaurant menu I was looking at on my phone earlier this week, and I could tell that they hadn’t set the width right for the menu items. They likely did it in percentages since they were really skinny and hard to read on mobile, but it looked good when I looked at the menu on a computer. I found it really cool to know the reason that the website looked the way it did.

If that’s what you find cool, you are my people.

This is definitely a class that can be taught well asynchronously because it is so online-focused anyway, so I would definitely recommend it to other GrC students.

Thanks for saying that. I’m glad it worked well.

I also have enjoyed learning about the design principles and seeing them in everyday life, especially “accesibility”.

I encourage you to browse the book for other principles that we didn’t cover in the class.

GitHub still confuses me as even though my websites work and load fine, a lot of the times on my images and HTMLs it has a red X, and says “checks not completed” which confuses me and makes me think something went wrong, even though it didn’t.

It happens, and it doesn’t seem to be your fault.

Image descriptions written during the proposal stage can later be used for my images’ alt text, making my design process simpler and the website more accessible.

Yes, don’t forget the alt text!

I would like to investigate how to add videos to a website next, that would be fun to play around with.

Look it up. It’s not that hard. Come to my office hours in the fall if you need help. Or if you want to share what you made!

Researching similar websites helped me give me inspiration for layout, navigation, and content organization.

Yes, that’s the idea.

I found some websites used interactive effects where images formed or animated as I scrolled down which was really cool. Even though those techniques are beyond what we’ve learned so far, they gave me ideas for what is possible in web design.

Yes, this class is just the beginning. Now you can go from here. Search engines have long been helpful with that, now you have the large language models to help you even further.

I am really proud of how much I’ve learned so far from this class. Before, I didn’t know a single thing about coding, and now I feel like I can build the basics of a website from scratch.

You should be proud!

I’ve been liking how the Design Principles and HTML and CSS assignments go together, and especially moving into this project and I am satisfied that I am instinctually considering everything we have talked about and learned in this class.

That’s awesome! I look forward to seeing your best work!
