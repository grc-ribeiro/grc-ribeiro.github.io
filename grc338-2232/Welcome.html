Welcome to GRC 338 (Web Development and Content Management). The class begins on September 19th and ends on December 9th, when the final exam will be due.

I know that several of you are very excited to learn HTML and CSS, while just as many are terrified of the subject, either because you had a terrible experience with it in the past or because you have had no experience with it at all. In all cases, I promise you that this class will be much better than you expect.

We will start from the beginning, and you will get to understand why you type every character that you include in your code. Web development is not magic, but you may just as well feel like a magician once you get the hang of it. I promise you that it will feel good to be the one in control.

GRC 338 is a hybrid class, where the lectures are online and asynchronous, while the labs will be in person. You are expected to work 12 hours a week in a 4-unit class, which means nine hours of work outside the lab. We have consistent deliverables every week. Please check Canvas constantly and stay ahead of schedule.

 

Tips to be successful in this class

  1. Have a schedule. My classes always have a consistent week schedule so you can plan ahead.

  2. Read the assignment instructions very carefully. I suggest you to read them sooner rather than later so you have time to ask the questions you may have.

  3. If you don’t understand the assignment requirements, ask; don’t assume.

  4. Avoid the mistakes from the list of mistakes to avoid. (Sounds pretty obvious, but those are, by far, the most common mistake in this class.)

  5. Show up to my office hours as needed. (In person or Zoom.)
