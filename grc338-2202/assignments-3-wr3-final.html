Website redesign

Using the layout from the last assignment, use media query as necessary to make it adapt to different sizes. Make sure that your media queries have a minimum width (i.e. min‑width: XXem).

Make sure to keep your HTML and CSS semantically organized. It’s not a bad idea to check with me if your choices make sense.

Use final content, image, style, etc.

  1. Start by reading my feedback from previous assignments, so you don’t make the same mistakes.

  2. Be very economical and follow these CSS restrictions.

  3. Try to reuse classes for consistency.

  4. Make sure to use appropriate, semantic class names.

  5. Make sure to use appropriate, semantic property values.

  6. Don’t forget to test in different screen sizes.

  7. Make sure to avoid the the simple mistakes. Each mistake from the list of simple mistakes to avoid will be a 20‑point penalty. Three or more mistakes is an automatic zero, with no further comments. It’s easy to avoid them all, though.

Beyond your technical skills on the HTML and CSS files, you will be evaluated on your capacity to apply the design principles that we have discussed in class to your work.

(You may want to redesign the logo of the company, but keep it very simple. I’d suggest its name on a nice typeface and one color. You won’t be evaluated on the originality and effectiveness of the logo. This is not a class on logo design. Just make sure it won’t be a distraction to your web design work.)

 

Evaluation

Design principles

  • Aesthetic‑usability effect; Form follows function; Expectation effect

    Were aesthetic decisions on the website guided by the appropriate language for the business it’s representing and its functionalities?
    10 points

  • Alignment; Hierarchy

    Does the placement of elements create a sense of unity and cohesion, contributing to the design’s overall aesthetic and perceived stability? Does the alignment of elements correctly lead the user through the page? Does the visual hierarchy represent the content hierarchy?
    10 points

  • Entry point; Visibility

    Does the home page conveys its job as the initial point of the website? Does it promote additional interaction? Are the main information and functionalities on each page clearly visible to the user?
    10 points

  • Color; Highlighting; Von Restorff effect

    Is color being used effectively to direct users to attract attention, group elements, indicate meaning, and enhance aesthetics? I’d suggest using tools such as  Adobe Color CC to achieve a harmonic color palette.
    10 points

  • Consistency; Similarity

    Are similar parts related? Are related parts similar? Are the variations consistently applied in all parts of the website?
    10 points

  • Picture superiority effect; Face‑ism ratio

    Are images being used to complement the information on the website? If you have images of people, is the appropriate face‑ism ratio being respected in all images?
    10 points

  • Proximity; Horror vacui

    Are related elements close together? Are close elements related? Are the amount of blank space on the pages appropriate for the business the website represents?
    10 points

  • Legibility; Chunking

    Is the text legible? Does the text have appropriate size, typeface, contrast, line‑length, and spacing? Is the information broken into appropriate chunks, so it is easier to process and remember?
    10 points

  • Mental model; Wayfinding; Iconic representation; Fitts’s law, Serial position effects

    Is the layout consistent with users’ expectations? Can users easily navigate through the pages of the website? Are the buttons easily understandable? Do the links follow a logical order? Are the buttons easily selectable, even if users are using their fingers on a touch screen rather than a mouse?
    10 points

  • Hick’s law; Progressive disclosure

    Are the possibilities of interaction limited to an appropriate number at each page? If there are too many options, are they broken into easily identifiable divisions in the page?
    10 points

 
Other principles, such as Recognition over recall, Errors, Forgiveness, and especially Accessibility must be taken in consideration, but they are covered by other principles and/or by the HTML/CSS part of the evaluation.
