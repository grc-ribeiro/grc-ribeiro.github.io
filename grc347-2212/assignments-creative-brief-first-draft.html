Creative brief (first draft)

As part of the process, you need to write a creative brief for your project. This should be one page or two at the most.

Requirements

  • Name of the brand

    This will be a line of products. What’s the name for the whole line. (If it was a car, it would be the name of the maker, not the model.)

  • Tagline

    A one line that encapsulate the essence of the product line. (It might not be as powerful as the ones in the video, but it should convey your brand voice.)

  • Target audience identification

    Don’t focus too much on demographics, but more on context. Rather than who, think about when. When would one choose to buy your product?

  • Products in the line of products

    You need a product line. What are the products you’d have? You need at least five different products, but three of them need different form factors for the packaging.

  • Product/brand essence and/or voice

    Describe your brand with adjectives about its “personality”.

Format

  1. Letter size portrait (vertical).

  2. Use a nice sans-serif typeface. Arial is nota nice a typeface.

  3. Please don’t use Gill Sans either. (Look up Eric Gill’s life in case you’re curious, but you can also take my word for it and believe me when I say that you don’t want to use a typeface designed by such a terrible person.)

  4. No colors on type. Keep it simple.

  5. Paragraph lines that are around 11 to 14 words long.

  6. Align to left, with no indentation.

  7. No hyphenation either.

  8. Use proper hierarchy. (e.g.: Bold for headings.)

A common mistake is not to work on its presentation, using defaults in InDesign or Microsoft Word.

Another common mistake is to go overboard with the design of the creative brief.

Just use simple, professional, good typography. Format it with as much care as if it was your resume or a cover letter. (I’m sure you learned it in GRC 318 or GRC 322.)
