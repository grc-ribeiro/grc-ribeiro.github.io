GRC 347: Resources

Class journal, week 1

It’s great to be taking another one of your classes again! Looking forward to another well-organized and focused class.

Thanks for the kind words, but please let me know as soon as you find something off. (Gina’s just emailed me letting me know that the video for this week was broken. Thanks, Gina!)

From reading the article, “Why is so much of design school a waste of time?” by Juliette Cezzar, I learned a lot about the importance of being able to receive critiques as a designer. I found the article very interesting, as it pointed out the importance of not only receiving critiques, but of actually making a point of seeking it out in order to learn more and grow as a designer.

It is a great article. When Professor Cezzar first shared it on Twitter, I promised her I’d assign it to all my future classes.

I was also wondering if we have to use the GrC virtual labs at all this quarter?

Nope.

I haven’t been a fan of Pinterest but I could not find any other websites that were competitively better, any suggestions?

I’ve never found any that I liked near as much as I like Pinterest. (If anybody has any recommendation, let me know.)

I loved creating the Pinterest board for packaging design ideas and I can’t wait to actually get started. I felt like I found a ton of really cool ideas on Pinterest whereas I had trouble finding them elsewhere (i.e. Google Images, articles) and I was wondering why you dislike Pinterest as a tool to find images as well as collect/organize them? I couldn’t find nearly as many unique or modern designs on other sites as the ones I found there.

I love Pinterest for saving images; it’s super easy to save them on my laptop or smartphone. I just don’t think it’s a good idea to look for images only on Pinterest. You may end up finding a very homogeneous set of references.

On the topic of being deliberate with aspects of your branding, I always found it helpful to constantly ask myself why I was doing something when designing a logo. Why is the logo circular? Why is the line weight thin/thick? It really helped me create a consistent look within the logo.

Yes, always ask why.

The competitive landscaping assignment was a great way to get started on this project! I was able to make a really helpful concept map for the category I chose that I know I will refer to a lot as we continue.

To design is to make decisions. Research helps you make informed decisions.

I also think that something like that is intentionally created so that it is a nice experience for the user, but isn’t something that is really obvious. I’m not the biggest fan of Apple’s products, but opening their phones always felt so satisfying, and now I’ll know part of the reason why.

And Hiroki Asai, who designed those packagings, is a Cal Poly alumn! (Also, super nice guy, I met him in 2016, when he came for a presentation on campus.)

I found it interesting that the more common, well-known brands did not have as intricate/exciting graphics as lesser known brands.

It’s really a shame, isn’t it?

I have been tasked with branding a recently developed center at Cal Poly; are we gonna learn about the branding process and how to effectively create a consistent brand? Just curious.

Yes. That’s a big part of this class. Start by doing a competitive analysis, then look for references.

It was cool to learn that hotels have signature scents and styles. It makes complete sense that they do.

Right? I think it’s cool too!

Not communicating anything is still communicating something! And every choice is deliberate! These two points I wrote in big letters in my notes because I find it to be the most valuable and overlooked piece of information.

Well, I’m glad you’re helping me emphasize them, then.

Nothing is completely neutral because everyone has their own voice and interpretation of things, so it’s best to be aware of your choices and intentional with your branding.

Yes, always be intentional.

I am really looking forward to this project and I think I will enjoy the whole process behind it.

I think you will!

The Ramones are a solid punk rock band, in all their leather jacket glory.

Hey ho, let’s go!
