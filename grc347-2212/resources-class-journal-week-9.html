GRC 347: Resources

Class journal, week 9

As the quarter comes to an end, I can’t help but think about what would’ve been different had this been in person. I know there’s no point in dwelling but it always hits me at around week 9, that learning by doing really is where I thrive.

I agree it’d have been better in person. But I appreciate how awesome you all were.

It was nice to meet during office hours this week and it helped give me more direction with my designs.

I really enjoy these meetings. I miss talking to my students in the lab.

Can I expand my line further than what I submitted last week?

Of course!

Currently, I’m experimenting with Adobe Dimension to get a better understanding of how the program works. I’m also watching videos about how to use Adobe Dimension. I’m looking into other softwares or websites that are similar to Adobe Dimension.

It’s only my second time teaching this class and I haven’t found the perfect solution. So I won’t be too strict about the prototypes. Please do whatever you find the best way to show your work.

I am having a little bit of trouble trying to create 3D renderings of my drawings because I am not familiar with how Adobe Dimensions functions. Placing the imagery properly is also somewhat difficult. Do you recommend any other rendering software to try out?

You may want to use some of the free prototype images you can download and place your label through Photoshop.

I managed to find some 3D models from Adobe Stock (though not perfect) and it’s satisfying to see the design look like real packaging.

I want to learn more about how a team at a company would go about designing a product together. How they interact and communicate their ideas.

It really varies a lot, especially now with so many digital tools available.

This project has been a very fun process with each step helping everything come together in a more thoughtful way.

That was my main goal. To design is to make decisions, and I hope you all feel better prepared to make decisions in the future.

I found the comments and feedback that I received from my classmates about my iterations to be super helpful as I worked on and refined my packaging designs.

I’m glad we have some peer-review process.

The peer-review system is really helpful and something that you should continue doing for your courses! It’s useful for me to know what my designs need work on, and I feel useful for being able to provide some feedback for my fellow students’ work.

Thanks for the feedback. I’ll use it when I need it, but I hope we can be doing it in person in the fall. But maybe a hybrid could also work? We’ll see.

I’m really happy with the project overall. It’s a lot of work but it’s enjoyable, and it’s rewarding to know that work is meaningful in granting me some real experience. It also made me realize that packaging is a sector of the GrC industry I could see myself getting into some day, too.

I have my interview for UGS Marketing Manager on Tuesday, any tips?

Just remember that whoever is interviewing you is on your side. They want you to do well.

If you have any free time, would you mind checking out my portfolio? I could use all the pointers I could get!

Will do!

I had a dream I was in Shark Tank, pitching my products which I find hilarious. Spring break couldn’t come sooner.
