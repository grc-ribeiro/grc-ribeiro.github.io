GRC 347: Resources

Class journal, week 5

I honestly struggled with creating the design of the package because I was not sure what to turn in and how to execute it.

My suggestion is to open the assignment instructions as soon as they open and shoot me an email asking for clarification before Thursday night.

I think I am going to investigate Dimensions some more because it really does help with the ideation process (even though it slows down my computer insanely).

We’ll use it later for presentation. But if you think it helps you with ideation, go for it!

I would like to investigate deeper into how to approach the creative process of creating a logo.

Investigating is not a bad idea, but compared to interaction design, the process is not as obvious, the heuristics are less defined, and the outcome is always more subjective.

It was a little difficult to get outside of my initial few concepts, but once I did I ended really liking some of them.

It’s almost like I know what I’m doing.

It was really hard to come up with a lot of different ideas for logos and such, I got stumped pretty often!

That’s why I gave you all the possible paths. Explore them without the fear to fail.

One of your comments was that I wasn’t referencing my references as consciously is you hoped. Once you commented that I remembered your example you gave in class about your process creating a logo and thought of how I could possibly improve my design.

I look forward to seeing it!

How can you tell if a logo is good or bad? Are all logos patented? Which type of logo do most businesses go with?

Logos are not patented but copyrighted. There’s no type of logo that most businesses go with. And there’s no official rules for good logos written anywhere.

I hope we can share our final mockups on a discussion board or something so we can see what everyone has been working on, that’s my favorite part of classes is seeing others progress and the final products.

We will. In one way or another.

Visual references can be super helpful when creating visual ideations, providing you with a jumping off point to start from, as well as the structure to keep the logo on track with your brand’s visual identity.

Absolutely! I never start a project without them.

I am excited to put more research and thought into ingredients and FDA regulations.

That’s a good idea. Each field has their own.
