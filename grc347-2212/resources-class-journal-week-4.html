GRC 347: Resources

Class journal, week 4

I do wish we had more of an assignment this week, as I didn’t find myself thinking about this class as much. But it was nice to have a break.

For some reason, I thought that more of you would struggle a little more with the creative brief. If I knew that the struggling was the exception rather than the rule, I’d have structured the schedule slightly different. But I guess it’s all good.

I love all the examples you show. Gets me thinking about possibilities for my own project.

Glad to hear. That was the goal afterall.

I’m excited to start designing-what do you look for in good packaging design? What softwares will we be using besides Illustrator?

Probably Photoshop and Dimension.

Will we be doing a critique with others?

I think we should. I haven’t yet thought this thoroughly through, though.

I’m learning to break the habit of only exploring and developing to one design concept. I’m trying to make sure I explore multiple design options and narrow down later on. We can use as multiple visual representations as long as they convey the brand voice.

I’ll help you with that by forcing you to go in different directions. Sorry, not sorry.

Will you be expecting us to get packaging printed, or will we simply be submitting mockups?

Digital mockups only.

What is your favorite tagline of an existing company?

I’m not sure if I have a favorite, but I’ll cite two credit card companies. I always remember American Express’s “Don’t leave home without it” and I find MasterCard’s quite cynical, but clever: “There are some things money can’t buy. For everything else, there’s MasterCard.”
