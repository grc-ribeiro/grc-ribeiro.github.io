Syllabus

GRC 347: Packaging Graphics Technology and Design

 

Course description

Advanced creative problem-solving strategies associated with the technologies used in designing graphics for packaging and output methods. Advanced techniques in graphic software including file hygiene, color management, and printing methods. Integrated packaging campaigns which include multiple substrates and digital storefronts.

 

Course objectives

Discuss how language, colors, customs, aesthetics, and placement affect global branding and packaging for products.

Explain the purpose and need of client design/marketing brief.

Develop brand story for package using appropriate design criteria and storyboarding.

 

Readings

Required book: Lidwell, William, et al. Universal Principles of Design: 125 Ways to Enhance Usability, Influence Perception, Increase Appeal, Make Better Design Decisions, and Teach through Design. The book can be read online for free through the Cal Poly library.

Additional online articles will be assigned on the class website.

 

Course expectations

The class is being held asynchronously, which means we don’t have a particular time to meet during the week. You are, however, expected to be engaged with the class during the week. Please check Canvas for all the deadlines.

 

Email communication

Please note that I only respond to emails that are professionally written, with correct spelling, and addressed properly. Though we are a pretty casual group in this department, please don’t address your professors by the last name only, because this is rude. I would prefer that you call me by my name, which is Bruno. Or, if you prefer the more formal approach, please call me Mr. Ribeiro or Professor Ribeiro, but again, mind the spelling.

Alternatively, you can send me a message through Canvas too.

 

Office hours

Since I can’t hold office hours in my office during this quarter, I’ll be available for office hours from Monday to Friday from 2:00 pm to 5:00 pm. Evaluation

A 100‑point scale will be applied to all assignments, with different weights, as indicated on Canvas. A 20‑point penalty will be automatically applied to late work. Another 20‑point penalty will be applied every 24 hours after the original due time.

 

Assignments

Quizzes: 20%
Every week, a quiz covering the content of the lecture will be assigned on Canvas. You are expected to take the quiz before you start working on your assignment for the week. You will not be allowed to submit it later, except for the rare cases of extenuating circumstances. Make sure to plan ahead.

Packaging design: 60%
A ten-week project will be assigned in the beginning of the quarter. Every week, there will be a different deliverable as part of the design process.

Final exam: 10%
During finals week, an online final exam will cover the content of the lectures. The exam is asynchronous with open book. Feel free to research as much as you need and even take in collaboration with another student.

Class journal: 10%
At the end of every week of class, you write a brief journal entry on Canvas. It should not take you more than five minutes, though. I will start every week by sharing with the class the highlights from the journal entries. Even though I will be able to know who wrote each entry, this information won’t be shared with class, and the entries will be shared anonymously. (Unless you submit a joke; jokes will be shared with credit to the student who submitted them.)

Plagiarism and originality

Please make sure the work you do for this courseis original and not a revised version of something you were working on in another course. Dishonesty, cheating, deception, or plagiarism is not acceptable. Unless otherwise stated and referenced in “fair use” copyright guidelines, it is expected that all design work be original work of the student.

Revising any existing work created by another person or student is plagiarism.

Revising any existing work created from another course is plagiarism.

If you have any questions about academic integrity in relation to your work in this course, make sure to discuss them with me and/ or consult the Office of Student Rights and Responsibilities (OSRR). Plagiarism and cheating may result in expulsion from Cal Poly and every university in the CSU system.
