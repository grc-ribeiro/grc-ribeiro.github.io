Class journal, week 6

I really enjoyed the assignment this week; I thought I had an idea of what my product would look like, but I found myself going outside the box. It was a good time.

I enjoyed reading about the different solutions you all have researched and presented.

The material research was also a good review from FSN 354 and how food and packaging work together.

Looks like you know way more about packaging than I do.

The mental model is so important for the products and services that we use today. It’s easy to subconsciously become frustrated when using an application that just doesn’t flow well because it tries to do things differently than other programs that we have become used to. It’s really impressive when innovative technology is released that users can learn how to use without becoming frustrated.

Apple is one that progressively trains us for some interactions before they become essential to using the product.

I really like to see the “form follows function” principle when it comes to architecture. I think that the most beautiful buildings are the ones where each design component has a practical function, and then those components are put together aesthetically.

I enjoy architecture examples too. I wich I knew more about architecture, though.

Keep in mind people’s mental models and expectations when thinking of shapes for packaging.

Yes. But also keep in mind that sometimes it’s beneficial to break those mental models. To design is to make decisions.

If environmental packaging is a good concern now, why haven’t many companies made changes?

Money.

Does multiple colors make packaging more costly?

You tell me. Didn’t you do your research? (They usually do cost more.)

Some unique shapes for packaging can be really cool and eye-catching, but costly to make since it may require a whole new process.

To design is to make decisions.

The middle of the quarter always brings back some bad procrastination habits for me; I really want to break that for the sake of meaningful classes like this one.

Week 7 is the hardest, but you can power through it!
