Packaging design

This project consists in designing packaging for a fictitious line of product from the list below.

  1. car products

    motor oil, polishers, etc.

  2. dairy products

    milk, butter, yogurt, etc.

  3. eye care

    contact solutions, contacts, etc.

  4. garden tools

    shovel, pruners, etc.

  5. hardware

    hammer, nails, etc.

  6. healthy beverage

    no alcohol or soda of any kind

  7. healthy snacks

    no sugar, or artificial sweeteners

  8. home cleaning supplies

    detergents, brushes, etc.

  9. innerwear

    socks, undershirts, etc.

  10. office supplies

    pen, paper, stapler, etc.

  11. oral care

    tooth brushes, toothpastes, etc.

Competitive landscape

As part of the process, you need to work on a preliminary analysis of the competitive landscape. You want to search for the main brands in your field as well as the not-so-known brands, with different, nicer visual language.

Use Pinterest or similar service to collect the images.

Important: Although, Pinterest is great for collecting images, it is awful tool for searching for images. Do not use Pinterest to look for your images. Find better sources and upload them to Pinterest yourself.

Include somewhere from 30 to 80 images on your board. (There’s no penalty for doing more, though.)

The competitive analysis count for 5% of your grade on this assignment.

PolyLearn: Submit the link to your image board until Monday, 30 September 2019, at 11:10 am.

Brand comparison and visual references

As part of the process, you will look for visual references to compare with your brand. Look for brands in other industries that “speak” the visual language that you want to apply to your brand.

You may also look for non-branded images with the appropriate look-and-feel of your brand.

Use Pinterest or similar service to collect the images.

Important: Although, Pinterest is great for collecting images, it is awful tool for searching for images. Do not use Pinterest to look for your images. Find better sources and upload them to Pinterest yourself.

Include somewhere from 30 to 80 images on your board. (Again, no penalty for doing more.)

The brand comparison and visual references count for 5% of your grade on this assignment.

PolyLearn: Submit the link to your image board until Monday, 7 October 2019, at 11:10 am.

Creative brief

As part of the process, you need to write a creative brief for your project. Include points such as:

  • Name of the product
  • Products in the line of products
  • Product/brand essence and/or voice
  • Package design objectives
  • Target audience identification and demographics
  • Product attributes and distinguishing characteristics
  • Competitive overview of rival brands and advantages

The creative brief counts for 15% of your grade on this assignment.

PolyLearn: Submit a PDF until Monday, 14 October 2019, at 11:10 am.

Visual ideations

Based on your research and visual references develop at least three digital options for one product of your line of products. (Still no penalty for doing more!) You want your ideas to be as different as possible. Explore different concepts, rather than different executions for the same concept. You don’t need to be constrained by physical attributes, yet. Think of a label, the front of a box, a view of a round tin, etc. The goal is to develop a visual identity rather than being too concerned with the specifics of applying to the package.

Visual ideations count for 10% of your grade on this assignment.

PolyLearn: Submit a multi-page PDF before your lab on Tuesday, 22 October 2019 or Thursday, 24 October 2019.

Material research and spacial explorations

Based on your research of materials, explain what material and processes you plan on using in your line of products. Explain the your decisions and make sure to include considerations on costs and environmental impact.

Include rough illustrations (sketches or digital) for possibilities on what the packaging will look like.

Material research and spacial explorations count for 10% of your grade on this assignment.

PolyLearn: Submit a multi-page PDF before your lab on Tuesday, 29 October 2019 or Thursday, 31 October 2019.

Packaging development

Watch the tutorials and, based on the previous two assignments, explore some possibilities for your packages.

The goal at this point is not to have a polished complete assignment, but to explore the software and your solution applied to a 3d model.

No deliverables. Have fun!

Line extension (first draft)

Extend your packaging to the other products in your product line. You may have a total of three to five products in your line.

You want to consider how to keep consistency throughout the line of products still making them distinguishable internally and agains the competition.

The first draft for the line extension counts for 5% of your grade on this assignment.

You can make them flat or three-dimensional, whatever makes more sense to your line of product.

PolyLearn: Submit a multi-page PDF until your lab time on Tuesday, 12 November 2019 or Thursday, 14 November 2019.

Line extension (second draft)

Based on critique and discussion during lab, refine the packaging design for all the products in your product line.

The second draft for the line extension counts for 10% of your grade on this assignment.

PolyLearn: Submit a multi-page PDF until your lab time on Tuesday, 19 November 2019 or Thursday, 21 November 2019.

First prototypes

Using the software of your choice, start working on applying your design to 3D models of your products.

The first draft of prototypes counts for 15% of your grade on this assignment.

PolyLearn: Submit a multi-page PDF until your lab time on Tuesday, 3 December 2019 or Thursday, 5 December 2019.

Final prototypes

The final deliverable for the project is a set of four to six boards with photographic representation or realistic rendering of your product line.

Include captions as necessary.

PolyLearn: Submit a multi-page PDF until Sunday, 15 December 2019, at 11:59pm..

The final prototypes count for 25% of your grade on this assignment.
