Tasks

  1. Extensively research and compare the two typefaces.

  2. Build a webpage built with HTML and one CSS external file explaining your findings to your peers.

  3. Make sure you have extensive comparison. Compare the most common letters, the most different letters, compare uppercase, numbers, and punctuation.

Requirements

  1. History of each typeface

    For each typeface, include facts such as who design it, when, and where. Also, explain if there was a specific purpose for its design and the historical context of its creation.

  2. Comparison

    Explain, through images and diagrams, what are the biggest similarities and big differences between the two typefaces assigned to your group.

  3. Examples and visual references

    Research images where the use of the typefaces were particularly successful. Fonts In Use can be a very helpful starting point.

  4. Style

    For the main text use a typeface that is very different from both typefaces you are researching. Suggestion: if your typefaces are sans-serif, use a serif typeface for your copy; and vice-versa.

Objectives

  1. To identify similarities and differences between similar typefaces.

  2. To develop best strategies to show such similarities and differences.

  3. To research the best styles that match the type families for future reference.

Style tips

  1. On your CSS, start with this line:
    h1, h2, h3, h4, p, ul, ol, li {margin: 0; padding:0; font-size: 1em; font-weight: normal;}
    (Watch the video to understand why.)

  2. Don’t use pure white background and pure black type. If you want your page to look like it’s black on white, use a very dark gray on a very light gray. (I use #333333 and #fafafa on this very page.)

  3. Don’t use default fonts. You probably have access to Adobe Typekit, use it.

  4. Give your page a max-width so your lines don’t get longer than 60 characters.

  5. Better yet, go ahead and download this template as a start point, please. Make sure you apply proper hierarchy on your CSS and add proper content to your HTML.
