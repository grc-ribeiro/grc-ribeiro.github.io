Even if it was boring, this was a good exercise. I didn’t know there was a table feature in InDesign, so this was good practice.

I know. I’m glad you all see the value of it. Sometimes, great design is almost invisible. Very few will value your ability to do a great design table, but those who do will be quite impressed.

Paragraph styles are powerful for creating tables. I disliked Futura for the tables in the beginning, but was able to easily change the typeface to Acumin.

Paragraph styles is what defines InDesign.

Pixie was a great help!

Thanks, Pixie!

Don’t divide columns with anything but space, for the most part.

Correct. Alignment takes care of that.

I hadn’t used the typeface Foco before but I like it a lot. Thanks for the font suggestions to look through!

That’s a cool one.

Nothing should be center aligned.

Seriously. Try to live by these words. It won’t always be desirable. But don’t center by default, center type as last resource.
