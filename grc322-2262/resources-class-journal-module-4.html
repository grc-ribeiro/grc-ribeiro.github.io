Class journal, module 4

I think this week’s project is a great way to get more passionate about design and looking at the trailblazers of the industry and how they went about graphic design and how they influenced the field.

That was the idea, of course.

Blank pages are a designer’s worst enemy.

Never stare to blank pages. Make something bad quickly and improve it until it’s not bad anymore. And keep improving it until it’s awesome.

I found that I really liked the exercise of only having 10 minutes to try and put a spread together. I think I will continue to practice this. It was also fun playing with the different possibilities of layouts.

It’s quite effective, right? Don’t be afraid of trying things fast.

Instead of working to completely finish one design at a time, quickly brainstorm/create a bunch of layouts and edit after. Try lots of new ideas and not slight variations of the same thing.

Exactly. Vectors are free.

Iterate, iterate, iterate. Through many drafts and tests the better outcome will show itself.

And keep asking yourself, “why is it good?” or “why is it bad?”.

The best starting point is throwing everything into the document and experimenting with layouts until you find one you like. Then, do the more precise adjustments.

Once you have everything there, you have something to improve upon.

The design poster is good practice to experiment with asymmetry and not defaulting to basic grid layouts.

Yes. Experiment. Fight your first idea. I mean, put your first idea on screen and move on the next.

I find it hard choosing fonts that pair well together.

That’s a tricky one. In the end, all attempts to make rules for that job have failed.

If you align to inside of the gutter… you are a monster.

Yes, you are.
