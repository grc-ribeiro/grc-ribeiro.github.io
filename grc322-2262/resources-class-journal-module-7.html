It was interesting to hear a bit about how Bruno works. I like doing paper sketches and it does help me map out what I plan on doing. Bruno, I learned, does not sketch on paper but does different ideas digitally. It is always neat to hear more about how other people work!

For many designers, sketching on paper is the fastest way to put ideas down. If it works for you, you should use it.

It’s good to take inspiration from the designers but not to the point where it looks like a straight recreation of their work.

Actually, you may start with a recreation of their work, and it will evolve to something else.

Pondering if assignments or projects can ever be truly perfect. I swear, there is always something that someone can point out to me that I could improve upon that I didn’t see before.

And that’s okay.

Put all of the content you plan on using on the page before working on nit picky details of your poster layout.

Once you have everything on the poster, you have no choice but improving its presentation.

It’s helpful to look at how fonts are being used in different media to get inspiration for our posters.

Yes. Finding good visual language references will make your design look much better.

I want to investigate more into font pairing. I watch a lot of design videos in my free time but I feel that font pairing is so much more subjective than it should be (also, doesn’t feel like there’s lots of youtube videos and resources on this sort of thing!) The only font pairs I really go for are serif + sans serif or slab serif + script fonts. I want to push this idea with my future work and the typography poster project has really expedited this feeling!

There have been many attempts to make font pairing a science, but it’s an art. And very subjective.

Although this is not super relevant to this project since we already have our “paired” fonts as it is a part of our project, this resource has been a great help for me for not only finding fonts that are paired well together but also using it as inspiration for how to pair my own fonts. https://www.fontpair.co/all.

Thanks for sharing, Lucas! More than guidelines, the pairing can be some nice suggestions.
