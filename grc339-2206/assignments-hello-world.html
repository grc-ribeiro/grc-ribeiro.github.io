GRC 339: Assignments

Hello world

For all the assignments in this class, you’ll be publishing your work on GitHub pages.

This assignment will serve two purposes:

  1. If you don’t know yet, you will learn how to publish your work on GitHub pages. (Follow the tutorial here.)

  2. I will have your GitHub account username, so you won’t need to submit your URL to Canvas every time. (If you took GRC 338 in the winter or spring of 2020, you probably have your GitHub account already. You may use the same, then. You’ll just have to create a new folder named grc339 with the new index.html file.)

Instructions

The assignment instructions are very simple, but it may take a little while for the few of you who have no experience with GitHub. (But don’t worry, it’s not complicated, just follow the steps on the tutorial.)

  1. Grab the following code:

    <!doctype html>  
<html>  
    <head>  
            <meta charset="UTF-8" />  
            <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1" />  
            <meta http-equiv="Cache-Control" content="no-cache, no-store, must-revalidate" />  
            <meta http-equiv="Pragma" content="no-cache" />  
            <meta http-equiv="Expires" content="0" />  



            &lt;title&gt;GRC 338: Hello world assignment&lt;/title&gt;  
&lt;/head&gt;   
&lt;body&gt;  
    &lt;h1&gt;Hello world&lt;/h1&gt;  
&lt;/body&gt;

    

</html>

  2. Publish it on the URL username.github.io/grc339/helloworld, where username will be your actual GitHub username. (Again, it’s all on the tutorial.)

  3. Submit your full URL to Canvas.

Due Monday, 22 June 2020, at 11:59 pm.

If you experience any issues, please triple-check your work and send me an email with screenshots of your repository and of your browser with the 404 error. Also, you want to go ahead and reserve a slot for my office hours on Monday.
