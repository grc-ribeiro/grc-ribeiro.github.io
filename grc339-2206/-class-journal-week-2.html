Could you give an example of what would be a second-level descendant?

Of course.

In the HTML below, header, article, aside, and footer are first-level descendants (or first-level children) of the div; while h1, nav, figure, h2, all the p, and all the section are second-level descendants (or second-level children) of the div.

    <div class="container">  
            <header>  
                    <h1>…</h1>  
                    <nav>…/<nav>  
            </header>  
            <article>  
                    <figure>…</figure>  
                    <h2>…</h2>  
                    <p>…</p>  
                    <p>…</p>  
            </article>  
            <aside>  
                    <section>…</section>  
                    <section>…</section>  
                    <section>…</section>  
            </aside>  
            <footer>  
                    <p>…</p>  
            </footer>  
    </div>

So if I make the div a grid-container, only header, article, aside, and footer become grid-items. The other elements will not be grid-items, because they’re second-level descendants of the grid-container div.

But if I apply the display: grid; declaration both to div.container and to header, then header will be both a grid-item (of the div.container grid) and a grid-container. In this case, h1 and nav become grid-items of the grid-container header, since h1 and nav are first-level descendants of the grid-container header.

Can you clarify why “It depends on the content of the second row, unless we define the height of the grid container element” is the answer to question number 5 (With the values 5em auto 3em for the grid-template-rows property, what’s the total height of the grid?) on Monday’s quiz and not the “100% - 8em” answer?

Since we didn’t define the height of the grid, it will be as tall as it needs to be to fit all of its rows. So it will be at least 8em, because the first row will be 5em and the third row will be 3em. But we don’t know the height of the second row, because it’s set to auto, which means that it will be exactly as tall as it needs to be to fit its content.

In the end, the height of second row will wind up being the total height of the grid minus 8em. This information is not only of very little help, but also not what was asked in the question.
